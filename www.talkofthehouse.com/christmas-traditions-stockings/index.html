Our stockings have enjoyed different positions in our homes over the years. We too hung them on our coat hooks in our entry in one home! I enjoyed that it was a little out of the norm, and gave you a little of the unexpected. Today they hang on our stairway along with fresh garland, white lights and beautiful ribbon. As a child we hung them on the door knob to the front door, and on Christmas morning we would find them leaning against the door, over flowing with fresh apples, tangerines and fresh nuts. I now understand and appreciate how the “fruit and nuts” began in our family. My Mother shared with me that during her childhood, her large family enjoyed simple Christmas gifts due to their limited budget. She said that it was somewhat a mystery how their small country home always seemed to smell of sweet apples and tangerines during the holidays. The sweet scent was everywhere, and seemed to grow stronger as Christmas approached. Years later she learned that each November her dad would purchase a crate of apples and tangerines for Christmas gifts for his children. The special fruit was stored in the bedroom closet which was adjacent to the double fireplace, which was the only heat source in the home. The fires burned daily, and the sweet fragrance filled the home as their ripening was hastened by the heat. Thus, my children enjoy fruit and nuts too in their stockings, in memory of my late grandparents and sweet, sweet memories!
