Valentine’s Day Gifts

print

Happy Valentine’s Day!   I have a quick post with all the gifts I am giving today.  Perhaps you can save one of the ideas and use it next year, (or if you happen to be a major procrastinator, you can use it today!) Let’s start with the gifts for my students.  They will get a ton of candy today, so I am giving them some cute red rulers (kind of helpful since we will be working on measurement soon.)

The teachers I work with in my grade level got chocolate gelato – with a spoon, of course.

You can see it better here.

My Atlanta-living son loves to travel and to eat pecan pie.  So here is his gift.  Little sister is going to be jealous since she is a big fan of pecan pies, too.  Both kids will be home this weekend to celebrate their dad’s birthday.

Middle child – the one who is traveling out west on his “adventure,” loves coffee and ice-cream, so Starbucks is perfect for him.  I will be saving his gift until he returns home, but he can see it here when he reads the blog.

I spent the evening making chocolate goodies for treats.

A plate of the chocolate dipped strawberries will be going to daughter – along with a cover for her tablet.

I also tried out Jenny Steffens‘ recipe for turtles.  They are good, but I think they would be better if the pecans were toasted in the oven with some butter instead of heated in a dry pan.

And here are the plates of treats ready to go to relatives.  They are not the prettiest in the world since I am soooo messy, but they are yummy.

Hope your Valentine’s Day is filled with people and treats that you love.  My day will be fun with 17 super excited third graders (and tons of candy!)

Happy Heart Day!
