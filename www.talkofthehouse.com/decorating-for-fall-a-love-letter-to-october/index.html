Decorating for Fall….a Love Letter to October

print

Dear October,

I must apologize to you.  I have tried my best to decorate with scary things for Halloween this month.  I really and truly did.  I wanted to have a party with all the cool items that are in the stores, and I even made shopping lists of things to buy for a fun fright filled themed party.

Oh yes.  I planned out a Haunted Mansion party, a Witches’ Ball, and even a warped Wizard of Oz one.  I thought of all the details that were needed for each… from decorations to menus to treat bags.  And then, do you know what happened every single time I got to the end of the planning lists?

My brain said, Nope.  That’s too scary.  🙁

I am sorry that I am such a big chicken and unable to commit to your major celebration like others do. But even though I apparently have commitment issues, know that I still love you and find you very inspiring.  You, my dear, are quickly becoming my almost-favorite month!

I love all the color you bring to the season…from the cheery pumpkins that take over the stores and farmers’ markets everywhere… to the colors on the changing leaves in the trees. In fact, I have found those bright oranges and reds and the pumpkins too irresistible.  They are what I embraced for my fall decorating. 🙂

I have used them all through the house here…from the foyer with its pumpkin and mum arrangement to a red tray filled with orange and red ribbon tied kraft bags full of candy for your trick-or-treaters.

I have used those colors on the old piano with a jar full of your favorite candy.

And I have tried to make the family room a place you would enjoy with its bright orange and red pillows…

and even a pumpkin one.
You do like that one don’t you?

Oh, and you like football too, don’t you October?

There are also drinks for you up on top of that cabinet.   Please help yourself. 🙂
(The ones in the basket are made with apple juice and have pumpkin spices in them.)

Now October, don’t get the idea that I only like how you look. Goodness no.  My love for you goes deeper than that.  You see, I adore how you feel.  You take away the energy zapping heat we have lived with since April, and you bring us your cool days and even cooler nights. Nights where we can snuggle up with blankets again.

And your cool weather makes it possible for us to once again enjoy the coziness of a fire in the fireplace.

The chill in the air makes us look better for you as well.  We put on our sweaters, and vests, and boots! We’re more fashionable.

And we cook better too.  But… I will wait to tell you about that the next time we are together because I know you are a busy one October, what with having to change the colors on sooo many trees and all that stuff. 😉

Just know that I think you are wonderful, and please forgive me for not decorating with all your scary Halloween decor. Maybe I can do better next year.

With love,
