Highlands’ Old Edwards Inn

print

source

With the exception of Acorns, I only showed you one side of the main street in Highlands, NC on my Weekend Wandering post the other day.  There was a reason for that, and today I want to tell you the story of the other side.  And I am not real sure how I feel about this story.  See what you think.

google.com

For many years, my sister and her husband would make an annual trip to Highlands and stay at the then charming Old Edwards Inn.  It was old. It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places, and it probably needed renovating at the time.  But it was right in the middle of downtown, and it had a nice restaurant…and a great outdoor dining area at which I frequently enjoyed a bowl of some of the best she crab soup I have ever tasted.  (This was long before I developed my allergy to shell fish.)

Anyway, someone else, or more likely, a group of someones, decided that they loved the Old Edwards Inn, and they also loved Highlands.  So they bought the inn and began investing millions of dollars in it.  It turned out beautiful.  They left the side with the old restaurant looking much as it did before the renovation – but better.

I know what you really want to see are the rooms, right?  Here are a few, and they are lovely.

 

oldedwardsinn.com

oldedwardsinn.com

They have not only renovated the entire inn, but they have also built a spa there.  I believe the last room above is one of the spa suites.  It has truly become a world class place to stay – with numerous awards given by the leading travel magazines.

Now here is where I don’t quite know where I stand on the story.  What they did with the Old Edwards Inn is a work of art.  It’s gorgeous.  But then…they began buying up other places in Highlands – and giving them their architectural “stamp.”  They bought and renovated the old Kelsey and Hutchinson place a block behind them and turned it into The Lodge at the Old Edwards Inn.  (We stayed there when it was K&H, and yes, it did need renovating.)

clarkzookarchitects.com

clarkzookarchitects.com

And yes it looks a zillion times better, too.

The group also bought up parcels of land downtown and built cottages that you can stay in.  They have the look of the Old Edwards Inn.

My favorite project that they have done is a mile or two outside of Highlands.  They have a house they call The Farm.  It can be rented for special occasions – mostly weddings.

So everything they have done is very beautiful and first class, but there are a few things about it that bother me.  First, I guess if all of these projects had been done by several different groups of investors, I wouldn’t feel as if it were a monopoly gobbling up the little town parcel by parcel.  And they are taking it parcel by parcel.  One of the oldest Christmas shops used to be housed in an old white building.  Here it is today after the Old Edwards Inn group left their mark on it.

weddingmapper.com

Yes, it looks like a nicer building, but it doesn’t look like the Highlands that I remember.  But should it?  What is progress?  And how is this any different than architectural covenants or resorts in other towns?  See that is why I can’t quite decide if this is good or not.

The other thing about it is it seems to be slowly changing into a town only for the rich. (I think it did start out that way many years ago.) If money were no object, I would choose to stay in one of their cottages, but for most of us, money is an object.  As each little parcel is put in their stash, the prices go up. I am afraid that eventually Highlands will no longer be a quaint little town, but instead, a resort for the wealthy.  That does bother me.

The other day, I showed you the other side of the street first so that you could get a feel for the old Highlands.  There is still this inn on that side, across the street from the Old Edwards Inn.  I hope they are able to keep their quaint charm.

destinationnexus.com

And there is this wonderful church on the same side of the street as the Old Edwards Inn.highlandsinfo.com

Surely they won’t gobble it up.

I have one more post for you on Highlands that I will share later this week…not quite as thought provoking as this one though. 🙂  It will be about our favorite place to stay there – and it is charming.

Meanwhile, I’d love to hear your thoughts on the pros and cons of development in an old town.

until next time,
