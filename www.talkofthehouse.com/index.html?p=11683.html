Summer Camp – A Magazine Photo Shoot Before and After and the giveaway continues

print

source

Yeeeeessss, I can hear you groaning…Not another Muskoka cottage, Kelly!…..I promise you this is the last post about them, but there are two really good reasons you are seeing them here one more time.  You see, I found these on two different sites as I was researching for the Summer Camp posts, and I thought it would be fun to compare the “normal” everyday rooms with the same spaces in magazine photo shoots. The second reason is because I have confused myself with these rooms, and I am hoping someone out there can help me. My husband thinks I need therapy. He’s probably right…. So let me start from the beginning….

Elmo Starr builders built this beautiful house (below) with its traditional Muskoka cottage exterior.

So far so good.

source

This is the porch that goes with that house as shown on the builder’s site.  Lots of beautiful dark wicker.

source

House and Home magazine featured that porch in its October, 2011 issue.  See the difference in the space?  They added oars in the corner, a throw on the ottoman, and a tennis racket by the plant.

houseandhome.com

I think they added a good many plants to the other end of the porch for the photo.houseandhome.com

Now here is where my confusion starts.  I found this photo of the boathouse (below) also on the Elmo Starr website (separate from the photo of the house above.)

source

In the same October issue of House and Home magazine (that featured the porch above) was this foyer…

houseandhome.com

and this dining room.
houseandhome.com

But… on the builder’s site with the photo of the boathouse, was this dining room.

source

Same room, right?  And if you look through the window, it looks like you can see the oval trimmed door on the porch from above – but no furniture.  So is this dining room in the main house and the boathouse just goes with it?  Or are these interior photos from the boathouse…as it leads you to believe on the builder’s website?  If you happen to have that House and Home issue in your possession, I would love to know so that I can be forever un-confused. 🙂

Okay, now back to the “comparing” part of this post.  Here is the living room as it is shown on the builder’s site (with the boathouse photo.) A boathouse couldn’t even have a fireplace, could it?

source

You can see how much was added for the photo shoot here…plants and candlesticks on the mantle, flowers, rearranged furniture, another throw, books.  (But we don’t know for sure if it may simply be that the builder photos were taken before the occupants had had a chance to decorate, and this really is the room’s “normal” appearance….or it may be that they styled it this way for the magazine.)

houseandhome.com

Looking from the living area to the dining room (from the builder’s site), you can get a better idea of how the space is laid out.  You can also see part of the kitchen in this shot.  I love the openness of this.

source

Here is the kitchen accessorized in the photo shoot.  Those rustic stools are a great “campy” accent in this lake house.

houseandhome.com

More for you to compare here… the upstairs landing from the builder’s site…

source

and the upstairs landing in the photo shoot.  This must be some time after the owners have moved in and accessorized.

houseandhome.com

This is the master bedroom from the builder’s site on the section with the boathouse.source

You can see sconces, a different bedskirt, and a throw pillow have been added in this photo from the magazine.

houseandhome.com

And finally, the master bath from the builder’s site…source

and the same room with a tub tray and other accessories from the magazine article.houseandhome.com

And finally, just a little reminder for you…Leave your psychoanalysis  comment and you will be entered in the drawing for the Summer Camp giveaway (that ends this Thursday, August 22, 2013 at midnight).  This little package of Found, Free, or Flea by Teresa Surratt, a blu-ray version of the movie, Indian Summer, and a set of skewers from Williams Sonoma for roasting marshmallows will soon be on its way to one of you so that you can enjoy your own summer camp! 🙂  (See more details here.)

As I am studying this post now, I am thinking maybe the builder didn’t want accessories in the photos on their site.  Maybe they wanted people to concentrate on the rooms themselves, and not the decorations…after all they were constructing rooms – not decorating them.  Okay, I am definitely overthinking all of this.  I need to be there on that dock..sitting in one of those red Adirondack chairs soaking up the sunshine (which we have had very little of for several days now.) Is there a difference in an Adirondack chair and a Muskoka chair? Be quiet brain…quit asking questions!  

Definitely need therapy….

cottagerental.com

Can’t wait to hear from you!
