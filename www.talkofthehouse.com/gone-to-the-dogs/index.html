Gone to the Dogs

print

source

I must admit I am not a University of Georgia bulldog fan.  I have been a Ga. Tech fan for as long as I can remember.  My brother and a dear friend of mine graduated from Tech, and I had a scholarship offer to attend there, but I do see the attraction to UGA.  My daughter spent this past Saturday afternoon “between the hedges” at her first UGA game with some friends.  This was her view in the sea of red.

I watched the game on television in between working on our sons’ bedroom, and oh my goodness what a game it was!!  It was a battle back and forth between Georgia and LSU all the way to the very end – with UGA winning 44 – 41…quite an exciting one to watch.  Even when watching football, you know my brain strays into the land of home design.  I thought it would be fun to take a look at some doggie decor today…starting with this cutie.

potterybarn.com

This coin bank from Pottery Barn would make a nice Christmas gift for a UGA fan, wouldn’t it? Pottery Barn also has these pillows that can be personalized with your pup’s name.

potterybarn.com

And I loved these St. Bernard appetizer plates they offered in their latest catalog.

potterybarn.com

They used to sale this great dog cubbie, but unfortunately it is not available anymore.

petsugar.com

Pottery Barn is not the only one to have dog themed items in their stores, though.  Take a look at this child’s bedroom from an old Restoration hardware’s RH Baby line catalog.

The dog artwork is no longer available, but those precious sheets can still be purchased.

rhbabyandchild.com

At one time they also had these great dog bookends.

source

Now let’s take a look at a few rooms that have a dog theme going on in them.  Isn’t that large piece of art in this child’s room wonderful?

mydesigndump.blogspot.com

Here is another bedroom for you to see.  Check out all the art and the throw pillow on the bed.

houzz.com

I have seen this wallpaper used in a number of rooms lately, but I really like how they added the accents of yellow in this particular space.

oharainteriors.com

We have looked at this next room way back when I did a post on entryways, but the dog artwork in it is too good not to include it here.

bhg.com

Not only do we find dog themes in room decor, but they are also popping up in children’s birthday parties everywhere.  Here is a peek at one of my favorite “dog parties.” (And there are even free printables on that site if you will click on the link below.)

onecharmingparty.com

You know I am a big fan of Susan Branch.  Did you know that she has a precious set of doggie cookie cutters available for sale on her site?  You could use them to make treats for your own puppy party! 🙂

susanbranch.com

And she has a beautifully illustrated recipe for real dog treats on her website, here. (It is near the bottom of that post, so be sure you go far enough down to find it.)

That brings us to the end of our post today.  I hope you have enjoyed seeing some of the many dog themed items and rooms that are out there.  As I have worked on our sons’ room, I contemplated using dog accessories in there but went in a different direction instead.  Fingers crossed I will be able  to show you some updated photos of that room this week.

onecharmingparty.com

And I will leave you with another question to talk about:

Do you have pets?  If so, what are they?  (We are definitely dog folks in our house.)

Looking forward to hearing from you!
