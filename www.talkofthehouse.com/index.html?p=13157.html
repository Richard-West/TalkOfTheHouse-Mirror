You are my Merry Christmas Gift

print
Merry Christmas! (I can officially say it now. 🙂 )  Last year, my last-of-Christmas posts was on Christmas Eve and included a link to this song from the Michael W. Smith cd It’s a Wonderful Christmas.


It’s a Wonderful Christmas

It was a very mellow, reflective piece – appropriate for Christmas Eve, but today was Christmas Day – a day for celebration!  So my music choice for you tonight has a very different mood – a mood of celebration, and it has become one of my favorites this holiday season.  Click here and enjoy another song from the same cd as you finish reading this Christmas Day post.

 

I hope you have celebrated God’s perfect gift with family and friends today.  This season has been a rushed one for me due to a too-full-of-events-November and a very late Thanksgiving, but I did not want to let the holiday go past without telling you what a tremendous gift your friendship here has been to me. From your comments to your emails…

and even to a little red sleigh with an arrangement of flowers sent to me by an anonymous reader this holiday season (which I used for the holly arrangement above when the roses faded), you have all been so encouraging of me and this blog.  I cannot thank you enough. I wish I could send all of you a Christmas gift!

There were several other posts that I wanted to do this month, but there were not enough hours to get everything in that my brain thought up for me to do.  So tonight, I am just giving you a peek at some of the spaces I wanted to share with you this holiday season that did not make it into a post.  I hope you enjoy them.

The boys’ bedroom had a small Christmas tree trimmed simply with kraft paper tags. I wrote holiday words on them with red marker and stuck the tree in a basket.

The porch was decorated for Christmas, too.  Brown paper packages tied with red ribbon sat in greenery atop the cabinet.

A new pillow from Pottery Barn was added to the swing.

The porch Christmas tree was trimmed again with pinecones, grapevine wreath garland, red berry garland, and balls of red berries and more grapevine.  I added some of the jute webbing that I used in the kitchen, but it seemed too large in scale, so I cut it in half to use on the tree.

You can see the nativity used again on the buffet on the porch.

I still like it out on the porch signifying no room in the inn.

I used the same centerpiece this year with other pieces from our nativity set, but I changed the napkin decor.

This year, I rolled up the napkins, tied them with some of the jute and twine, wrote a name tag on black cardstock, and stuck in a little greenery cut from the tree.

And that brings us to the end of my Christmas post for you.  I hope you enjoy the rest of your holiday with loved ones you hold dear.

Merry Christmas to you.
