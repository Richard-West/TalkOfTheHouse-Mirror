Weekend Wandering in Highlands, NC

print

source

A lot of people are traveling this weekend, so I thought we would take a little virtual trip here over the next few days to my favorite small town – Highlands, North Carolina.  Many many years ago, my then boyfriend (my now husband) and I drove into Highlands, and I fell in love with it.  I know parking is a nightmare, but how can you not love this place when this is what you see when you drive up that crazy curvy road from Georgia?

ncmountainlife.com

And this is what you see when you come in from another crazy curvy way. With the claim as the town with the highest elevation east of the Mississippi, every road is “up” and curvy to reach it.

visitwestnc.com

There are beautiful views of mountains and waterfalls no matter which way you go or come to Highlands.

flickr.com

The downtown is filled with charming little shops – which is why the parking is such a nightmare. (You won’t find a Walmart or a Target here.)

theoldpostroadblog.blogspot.com 

I have loved it even more when I have been there in the snow.

highlandsinfo.com

Among their many shops is Wits End – a wonderful women’s clothing shop with a few pieces of children’s apparel mixed in.  Next door to it is the cozy Buck’s Coffee Cafe where you can pick up a cup of coffee and dessert while listening to live music.

highlandsinfo.com

Further down from them is my favorite little bookstore – Cyrano’s.  Its 700 square feet are wall to wall jampacked with books, and there is even a children’s section in the back.

highlandsinfo.com

There are numerous art galleries, antique shops, and a toy store.  And there are even two Christmas shops!! But my very favorite place for home items and antiques is Acorns.

weddingmapper.com

Here is a peek at the inside of the shop.  Upstairs carries women’s clothing and accessories.weddingmapper.com

Across the street from Acorns is a store called The Dry Sink.  It is filled with everything you could want for your kitchen plus lots of gift items.

thedrysink.com

When the children were growing up, we would also stop in at this deli to get sandwiches, ice cream, and drinks when we were in Highlands.  It was very crowded inside so we would sit outside on the benches and eat (and people watch.)

whiteoakrg.com

We haven’t been to Highlands in over a year, so I was surprised to see that the owners of the deli have retired.  The property sold for a ridiculously low price (wish we had seen it sooner.)  I do not know if the new owners have opened it again.  I would bet money that the Old Edwards Inn company has bought it.

While we are on the subject of eating, I wanted to share this with you.  A number of years ago, we decided to forego the day of double eating with our relatives and instead, spend Thanksgiving in the mountains.  It has remained my favorite Thanksgiving memory (sorry relatives.)  We made reservations for the Thanksgiving feast at the Log Cabin Steak House in Highlands.

logcabinevents.com

The restaurant was in an authentic Joe Webb designed log cabin.  We dined in the end of that enclosed porch area.

logcabinevent.com 

The entire cabin was beautifully decorated.logcabinevents.com

All of our food was delicious (except the children did not like the bourbon pecan pie!)  The highlight of the holiday was when it began to snow as we returned to our cabin.  What a fun and gorgeous surprise to have in late November!

The Log Cabin Steak House has closed, but it has been reopened as Rustico which is opened only seasonally.  I look forward to dining there the next time we are in Highlands (although nothing will compare to the memory of that Thanksgiving in the former restaurant there.)

source

And my final thing to love about Highlands in this post today is the town has numerous cut-your-own Christmas tree farms all around it.  So if you are traveling in that area over the Thanksgiving holidays, be sure to get a fresh Frasier fir or spruce (or some other variety) for Christmas – or at least pick up a wreath from one of the farms.  They smell heavenly!

bestfreshchristmastree.com

I’ll be back next time with some accommodations that I think are beautiful in Highlands.  Until then, I will leave you with a question…

If you could spend Thanksgiving anywhere with your family, where would you choose to go?

love to hear from you!
