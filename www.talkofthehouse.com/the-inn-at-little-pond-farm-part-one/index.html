The Inn at Little Pond Farm – part one

print

If you were reading the posts here last week, you learned that my sweet husband took me on a vacation over Labor Day weekend to celebrate my birthday and our anniversary – a trip he alone planned.  I had said earlier in the summer that I really wanted to see the Cashiers Decorator Showhouse this year, so when he gave me tickets for the tour and announced that he had made reservations at an inn in North Carolina, I assumed it would be somewhere in the Cashiers or Highlands area.  He told me it was going to be a surprise, and it was definitely a surprise.

After the tour, I thought for sure we would head back to Highlands and check in here…

The Inn at Half Mile Farm.

or perhaps our reservations would be for here…

The Old Edwards Inn

But when we left the tour we went in the opposite direction from Highlands, and we drove and drove and drove.  My geography skills for the state of North Carolina are not very good, so I had absolutely no idea where we were going.  Finally, I saw a sign with Asheville on it, so I thought, “Oh yes…I know where we are going… We are headed to Maggie Valley to check in here…”

Cataloochee Ranch

the place we spent our honeymoon.  But we didn’t head to Maggie Valley.  No…we kept on driving and driving and driving. Finally I saw a sign for Boone which made me think of Blowing Rock, and that made me think of Valle Crucis.  So I then narrowed it down to either…

The Mast Farm Inn

or…

The Inn at Little Pond Farm

We passed Mast Farm, and that left only one option.  So three minutes from the inn my husband finally confirmed my guess. He reads the blog and said that it was easy to choose a place to go…just choose an inn that I have written about. 🙂  We had never stayed at the Inn at Little Pond Farm before, but after our visit,  I must say, it has to be the nicest place – by far – that we have ever had the pleasure of staying in.

Your tires crunch on the gravel as you pull into their drive.  When you get out, you immediately smell that evergreen scent of the North Carolina mountain region, and you can see the pond across the road.

The Inn is a renovated farmhouse with a large addition that has been built on.  They looked for a place for 6 long years before finally purchasing this one.

The porch and exterior are your first clues to the simple luxury awaiting inside.

We entered through a middle set of doors into this gorgeous room.  I cannot even begin to describe the quality of the craftsmanship in the inn. Frank and Gaye Luaces are the owners, builders, cooks, managers…the everything of the inn.  They have literally poured themselves into it – and with it tons of talent.  Gaye is the visionary and Frank is the man to make it come to fruition.  He built it all.

I mean everything…the walls, the ceilings, the cabinets, the shelves, the doors.  His talent is amazing.  Do you see the  bar below?  He designed it so that the front part wheels out separately, and the unit can be used by a bartender for the weddings they host at the inn.Gaye is not short on talent either.  She comes from an interior design background, so the colors and designs of the rooms are all her styling.Her dream of owning a bed and breakfast inn began with a culinary program.  They host classes with chefs weekly in the huge gourmet kitchen – designed specifically for this.  It is a major part of the inn at Little Pond Farm.

Isn’t it beautiful?!  A class was about to begin as we arrived at the inn.  We were not signed up for that evening’s event, but Frank and Gaye graciously offered us drinks and snacks as they helped us take our belongings to our room.

Then they told us to feel free to explore the rooms.  So we enjoyed our drinks and toured the rest of the inn (with camera in hand!) as they finished prepping for the night’s class. They are such wonderful hosts, you feel as if you are spending the weekend with friends.

This huge table is where all of us gathered for breakfast each day.  Gaye’s collection of dishes is astounding!

There is a smaller dining area if the additional space is needed (and it is when there are weddings or other special events held there.)And there is even a quiet little table set up for two if desired. And look…another cabinet of dishes! Frank designed and built those cabinet doors, too.The fireplace is original to the old farmhouse.  Gaye has a very “less is more” style – evident in the living area.The bank of windows in this reading room looks out on the grassy lawn.There is also a smaller kitchen in this area that can be used for preparing breakfast for guests when Gaye doesn’t need the big kitchen. (I would be more than happy with this little room and its walk in pantry!)
More craftsmanship for you to see…just look at the staircase and railings (and the walls!)  Frank says he used poplar for almost all the surfaces.  Once he finished building everything (which took years and years and years) a team of workers came in and puttied every single nail hole over a period of 6 weeks and prepped the rooms for painting.  Then someone came in and spray painted the entire interior.The paneled area below the stairs once led to the basement of the original farmhouse.  It now has a different entrance, but the panels disguise a closet area below the stairs (almost like a secret room!)

Our room was on the first floor of the inn, by another stair case.  This small beverage area was right outside our door, and the little refrigerator was stocked with drinks for our use.

In my next post, we will tour the guest rooms at the Inn at Little Pond Farm.

It is such an amazing place, I hope you will be back to see more.See you next time!
