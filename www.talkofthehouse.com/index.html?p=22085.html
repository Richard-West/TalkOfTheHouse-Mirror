Road Trip: 2015 Southern Living Idea House part 2

print

Welcome back road trip travelers!  For part 2 of our tour here, we are going to take a look at the bedrooms and baths that Bunny Williams did for the Southern Living Idea House near Charlottesville, Virginia. (If you missed part 1, just click here.)  We saw everything downstairs in the last post except for the master bedroom area.  I love that this room has a door out to its own porch. (But don’t hate me…I am saving all the porches for part 3 of the tour.)

The blue and cream color scheme seems very restful, but it is livened up with large works of art.

And of course, all dream master suites have a fireplace in the room. 🙂

Off the master bedroom is the master bath…again in blues and creamy whites.

That is my kind of soaking tub!

There is a lot of built in storage in this room.

And a gigantic walk in shower.

Off the master bath is the even more gigantic master closet with so much storage space, I can’t imagine having it all filled.  But then again, there might have only been one piece of furniture in the bedroom itself that would hold any clothing, so all clothes would have to go in here…

and shoes, and accessories, and jewelry.  But obviously it could handle it all.

I love that there is a window in the closet! 🙂

Now that we have finished everything in the master suite, let’s go back in the foyer and climb the stairs.

Here is a view looking down at the foyer.

The upstairs is designed for the “children in the family.”  This big area is at the top of the stairs, and all the bedrooms come off of it.

It is called the “pajama room” and is a general hang out area for watching tv, playing video games, putting puzzles together…you name it.

I really liked the cabinet with the glass top area for the television, but do you see any way to open the “doors” at the top? Or is that just for looks?

Isn’t that a great vintage Coke sign?!

One of the hallways off the pajama room leads to a girl’s bedroom and bath.  You come to the bathroom first.

A braided rug…what’s old is new again. 🙂

And this is the girl’s bedroom.  It seems a bit sophisticated to me for a teenage girl, but perhaps they would like it.

Have you noticed the use of silhouettes in the house?

The window seats in this room are wonderful!
(Along with that bead board ceiling.)

Now let’s go back to the pajama room and off in a different direction.  You will enter the bunkroom with its great built in beds.  This one seems more kid-friendly to me.

More use of bead board.

All the bedrooms upstairs have charming angled ceilings.  It makes for nice little nooks to fit in seating areas…

and study spaces.

There is another bathroom on the other side of the pajama room with more built in storage. (This house really has great space for storage!)

Here you will find a super large walk in shower with a built in bench.

And one last bedroom to see upstairs – my favorite of the bedroom spaces. 🙂

This is the “boy’s room.”
That rattan bed is a beauty!

On one side is the desk area with a cute vintage cowboy print above it…

and on the other – a dresser.  You can see from this photo that you have to go down a few steps to access this room.

There are window seats (with drawer storage) on both sides of the room.

And that finishes our tour for today.  I have saved all the outdoor spaces to share with you in part 3.  They are absolutely my favorites of the house.  You are going to LOVE them (and there are a number of them!)  Plus there is one bonus room for you to tour as well. 🙂

So I hope to see you back here soon!

Until next time,
