My Favorites – Phoebe Howard Part 3

print

source

It’s Saturday.  I know many of you are looking out your window and seeing snow.  Don’t hate me.  It will be 80 degrees here today (and it is sunny)- yes, in the middle of January.  In this final post of Phoebe Howard rooms, I wanted to share my favorites with you.

phoebehoward.net

It seems that many of my favorite rooms are in vacation homes – or they are at least in locations that many people go for a vacation.  So I want to help you escape the cold and go on a virtual vacation here today. Wouldn’t it be great to be sitting on one of these porches?

phoebehoward.net

The one at the very top of the post is in Atlantic Beach, Florida.  The two shown here with that lovely shade of blue green are from the 2011 Coastal Living Idea house Phoebe Howard decorated in the East Beach development of Norfolk, Virginia, and this is the cute stairway from that same Idea House.

phoebehoward.net

Now let’s travel from East Beach up to East Hampton to the Howards’ personal home.  I love this guest bedroom with its old flag on the wall.  It seems perfect in this location.

phoebehoward.net

And one day I want to make a headboard using a quilt like they did for this room.  Isn’t that gorgeous?!

phoebehoward.net

The kitchen in their home has a contrast of painted and stained wood cabinetry, and glass doors are in the mix, too…all my favorites.

phoebehoward.net

Let’s go back south now to another beach location – Sea Island.  Here is the kitchen Mrs. Howard has done there with very similar elements – white painted cabinets, some with glass front doors, mixed with a stained wood island.

mrshowardpersonalshopper.com

Now I am going to take you in a different direction on our little virtual vacation here.  We are going to leave the beaches and head to the mountains.  My all time favorite home that Phoebe Howard has done is this one in Cashiers, North Carolina (not far from Highlands).

housebeautiful.com

It has been featured in House Beautiful magazine and pinned over and over on Pinterest.  So you have probably seen it, but I can’t leave this home out if I am doing a post on my Phoebe Howard favorites…and this next room is my favorite of the favorites.

housebeautiful.com

The colors, the prints, the plank walls, the big window with the long simple drapes, and the mantle…!  I could easily plop down on that sofa and feel right at home.  Couldn’t you?  It makes me want to change the arrangement of the furniture in my great room, and the repeat of the simple accessories on the bookshelves makes me want to edit edit edit ours.

cotedetexas.blogspot.com

Now take a look at this staircase with its plank walls…the windows…the gallery of framed prints, and look up as you enter it.  The ceiling has painted planks, too.  Simply gorgeous.

phoebehoward.net

This is the bedroom hallway.  I am not normally a fan of antlers, but it does work here…and they are a common theme mixed with transferware in the Highlands/Cashiers area of North Carolina.

housebeautiful.com

The plank walls and ceilings continue in the master bedroom with its soft bed linens and drapes.  This room really has a cocoon like feel…something that would be needed in the cold weather in that mountain region.

housebeautiful.com

One of the guest rooms sports my favorite fabric for its drapes – buffalo checks.

housebeautiful.com

And finally, we will end like we began… with a porch.  I have shared these two photos on another post about porches, but since they belong to this wonderful house done by Phoebe Howard, I am sharing them again. (Forgive me for the rerun.)  This porch is definitely one of my favorites with its wicker furniture, neutral colors, and amazing view of the Appalachian Mountains.

phoebehoward.net

That brings us to the end of the Phoebe Howard mini-series and the end of our virtual vacation today.  I hope you have enjoyed the range of rooms that Mrs. Howard has done, and if you are snowed in, I hope this little “trip” has brought you some virtual sunshine. 🙂

phoebehoward.net

I swear it looks like late spring here with the sunshine, light breeze, and warm temps.  I just heard the screen door slam as my son headed outside to play basketball, and I am sitting here in the dining room with the doors wide open.  Crazy for the middle of January!

Enjoy your weekend.
