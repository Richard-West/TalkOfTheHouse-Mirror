I’m Dreaming of a White Kitchen

Nantucket Home Traditional Home Magazine

I am getting together a post on all the changes to the kitchen in this house, but it is requiring some digging to find the “before” pictures – since there have been a zillion transformations of it in the 16 years we’ve lived here. (The one above is unfortunately not my kitchen.)  So while I am in the kitchen-looking frame of mind, I thought I would entertain you with some photos of some other kitchens, and I will keep digging through the photo boxes.  I am going to start with white kitchens and save the ones with color for another time.  Let’s take a look at some of these beauties.

I am not a big fan of the baskets in this one, but those windows and the island with open shelving make me jealous.  I wonder how that lamp plugs in ….. hmmm….a hole in the island countertop?

Historical Concepts Good Housekeeping September, 2009

There’s lots to love in this one….the floors… the upper doors with glass… that island!

Historical Concepts BHG August, 2008

Or how about this small one?  That countertop…gorgeous!!

Historical Concepts Home October, 2004

This has been one of my favorites since it appeared in Southern Living a while back.  It just looks so lived in and has classic details.  The lighting here really makes it for me (remember I am addicted to light 🙂 )…double sconces, hanging pendants….

Southern Living Jamie McPherson

There is a lot going on in this next one, but I wanted you to see the ceiling and the way the island is designed. Isn’t that smart?

Architectural Digest

The ceiling design on this one is also unusual, and I really like those glass door cabinets that face each other…another idea worth copying.

Traditional Home Magazine

More cabinets with glass doors…and more wooden countertops…

Country Living Magazine

No glass doors here, but beautiful open shelving and there’s that wooden countertop again.

Country Farmhouse Country Living

Maybe these can hold you over for a little while.  If you are like me, pretty kitchens are always candy for the eyes…love ’em!

Until next time…
