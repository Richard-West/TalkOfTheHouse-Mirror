Road Trip: Perkins Cove, Maine and the Marginal Way

print

A number of years ago, my husband and I made a trip to Kennebunkport, Maine –  complete with drives along rocky coastlines and lighthouse tours.  I would have happily stayed at the Cottages at Cabot Cove again, but there was no availability for three people for our dates.  That sent me into research mode, and the inn I finally settled upon was not in Kennebunkport.  (You are going to love it though!) So this trip was to be a little different from our earlier one, but there were a few repeat places I wanted to be sure we took our son to visit. The places we are looking at today are some of those – good enough to keep coming back to! 🙂

source

On a Sunday morning we took a break from working on our son’s apartment and left New Haven, Connecticut  heading north.  By lunch time we were making the turn off of highway 1 to get to Perkins Cove. We parked the car, hopped out, and made our way down the flower lined sidewalk…

to Barnacle Billy’s.  We had dined there on our previous trip, and the views made me want to visit it again.  There are actually two Barnacle Billy’s – the original, which has a somewhat limited menu (with a lot of lobster) and Barnacle Billy’s Etc. with a more diverse selection of dishes.  We chose the latter and waited for a few minutes for outdoor seating.  (I was more than willing to wait for that. 🙂 )

tripadvisor.com

This was the view from my seat.

See why I wanted to wait for a table outside?

Across their garden is the original Barnacle Billy’s, and I’m sure the view from its deck was equally beautiful.

I could watch all the boat action there in Perkins Cove from our table.  And by the way, if you are visiting in the area,  you can take chartered rides for sightseeing or fishing.

More garden views…

You could also see some of the shops in Perkins Cove from our table.

We ordered, and our food arrived shortly.  Barnacle Billy’s is famous for their lobster.  I don’t remember what my husband chose, (probably haddock) but our son had the lobster roll.  I did not have a shellfish allergy years ago when we visited, and I know I loved that plate full of deliciousness!  Our son, on the other hand, did not care for “the texture” of lobster, 🙁 so that ended his experience with lobster rolls for our trip.

My burger.


After eating way too much, we needed to walk off some of that food before climbing back in the car for more riding.  We left Barnacle Billy’s.  (Do any of you know what these very tall yellow flowers are?)

*Edited on 9/4: Mystery solved!  It is a cup plant that grows up to 8 ft. tall and has sunflower like leaves. When it rains the leaves hold water in little “cups” (which birds love) hence its name. It is native from Maine to all parts of….Georgia! So I am going to be on the lookout for it here.  Thank you for the info Leigh!

We strolled down the street past Oarweed.  I think on our next visit I want to try some of their dishes.  They do not fry any of their food, (which is actually safer for me with shellfish allergies) and more importantly, they have a great selection of desserts. 🙂

Beside Oarweed you will find this sign.  It is the entrance to the Marginal Way, a 1 1/2 mile long old Indian trail.

We followed the sidewalk around the side and to the back of the restaurant.

I couldn’t help gawking at their window boxes overflowing with gorgeous red geraniums!

But this is what I was supposed to be looking at – the view of the water. 🙂

That rocky coastline… (It is really a long way down, but you cannot tell it from the photo.)

Our son just had to venture down so that he could get in the water. Boys!

The Marginal Way is an easy stroll along the cliffs with amazing views.  There are even 39 benches scattered throughout if you should need to take a rest or just want to sit and relax for a while.

marginalwayfund.org

In the first part of our walk from Perkins Cove, there were houses on our left side, but they were well hidden from our view by their landscaping.  About halfway, we rounded the curve and saw this….

At this point the houses became quite visible, and the walk continued right past them.  And at this point my crew decided it was time to head back to the car because we had further to travel that day. 🙁

living-in-stuutgart.com

The entire area around Ogunquit and Perkins Cove is simply charming – filled with restaurants, art galleries, shops, and many many inns – along with beautiful houses.

 You could easily spend a week here.  On our next Maine trip, perhaps we will stay at the Parsons Post House Inn so that we can truly enjoy the area without having to rush to leave.

parsonsposthouse.com

The rooms are all done in restful blue and white.

parsonsposthouse.com

And their bathrooms look perfect

parsonsposthouse.com

On our previous trip, my husband and I made the 20 minute drive over to the Nubble Lighthouse, but I regret we didn’t do it this time.  We had another stop that was going to take a couple of hours before we were to check in at our inn for the night and a couple more hours on the road as well.  If I had known that we would only see one more small lighthouse on this trip, I think I would have insisted we make the drive to Nubble.  It would have been totally worth it.

flickr.com

But the two hour stop beckoned and we drove on.  I was planning to share it with you in the next post, but I am going to have to change my schedule a bit.

There is a deadline on the calendar, a giveaway you will want to enter, and a place that you do not want to miss in the next post. So…I am jumping out of order on the road trip.  We are going to skip a few stops and look at something from the very last day in the next post.

I hope you will be back to see all of it then!

Until next time…
