Len Berg’s Restaurant and that Macaroon Pie

print

source

One of the places my husband’s family liked to eat when he was growing up was Len Berg’s restaurant in Macon, Georgia.  It was only natural then, that he should take me out to eat there when we were dating.  It wasn’t much to look at from the outside…a low brick building with striped awnings stuck down Post Office Alley.

source

But I loved Len Berg’s – as did many other people – the folks who worked downtown, people traveling through Macon, families there to celebrate a special occasion.  It was an institution.  When you entered through that green door, you walked into a small room with a drugstore type counter lined with red stools facing a wall full of cooking tools.  The scene was lively with diners joking there, the waiters rushing about with trays of food, customers talking with each other.

source

You could sit at the counter to dine, or you could follow the crazy labyrinth of tiny rooms and be seated at a table.  If you were lucky (and we often were 🙂 )you would get to sit in one of their cozy wooden booths with a little sconce in the wall that gave just enough light to read their daily menu.  The food was traditional Southern fare…black eyed peas, corn sticks, turnip greens, squash. My husband would usually get the salmon croquettes.

source

I have no idea what meat or vegetable I used to get, but just like my father in law, I loved their orange colored blue cheese dressing.  It was like a combination of French with blue cheese, and it was delicious. My father in law and I would gobble up their salad because of the dressing, and we also devoured their desserts.  There always seemed to be lemon meringue pie on the menu, and around the first of June you would see a billboard advertising  “H.M.F. P. I C. – You know where,” meaning the homemade fresh peach ice cream was available – made from locally grown peaches.   But those were not what we wanted – nosirree – my father in law wanted their famous Hot Fudge cake, and I wanted their equally famous macaroon pie.

source

I would wash it down with a Coca Cola served in one of those little glass bottles.  Years later, they modernized and began serving them in cans.  After your meal, when you would leave through that green door, there was a buzzer that you could press.  Its sound was a message to the cooks that you enjoyed your meal.

Len Berg’s opened in the early twentieth century, and it changed owners a few times during the almost 100 years that it remained in business.  Each time it changed hands, the food still remained the same though. After we had children, we would take them to eat there on Friday nights – introducing them to orange colored blue cheese dressing, salmon croquettes, and macaroon pie.  They would press the buzzer to let the cooks know they liked their food.  Sadly, the restaurant closed a number of years ago.  And so my search began for recipes for their blue cheese dressing and macaroon pie.

Look what I found late last fall…

Remembering Len Berg’s Restaurant  (Amazon Affiliate link)

I was Christmas shopping in a little store in Macon, and it was behind the counter.  I was so excited, and I asked the clerk, “Is that a cookbook from Len Berg’s?!”  She said, “Yes and no.  It has all the history of the restaurant and about 30 recipes.”  I asked to look at it.  (You know what I was looking for. 🙂 )Sure enough they were there…recipes for the blue cheese dressing and the macaroon pie (and the salmon croquettes, too.)  I immediately bought it and on my way home decided that it would be the perfect Christmas gift for my father in law.  So for Christmas, my husband and I made up a large batch of the salad dressing, a macaroon pie, and gave those to him with the book and a few other things from the book.  He loved it (and he said all the history of the restaurant was interesting reading, too.)

The salad dressing recipe in the book makes a lot, but I have found a similar recipe that makes a smaller quantity.  I thought you might like to try it.

Hope you have enjoyed my memories of Len Berg’s restaurant and that you will try out one of the recipes. It’s back to work for me tomorrow after my week of spring break (more like spring cleaning…and I still have two more rooms to go!).  I think I will pack a slice of pie for my Monday lunch – if there is still any left.  Hope your week gets off to a good start!
