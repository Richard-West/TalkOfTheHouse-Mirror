Clocks, Calendars, and a Touch of Blue … Decorating for the New Year

print
The Christmas music is still playing around here, and the greenery and wreaths are still hanging outside, but the big tree has been un-decorated and taken out. ¬†I would have loved for it to stay up longer, but my husband offered to help with it this past weekend. ¬†Since it is such a major job, I gladly took him up on his offer. ¬†Is your tree still up? ¬†With ours gone, I was able to start thinking about decorating for New Year’s Eve.

I mentioned to you in the last post, that I was adding some blue around here. ¬†Last year when all the red of Christmas was removed I wanted only black and white in the rooms. This year, it seems I want the calmness of blue. So I moved some blue books onto the foyer buffet. (And just so you know, that is not champagne in the bucket…it’s sparkling cider. üôÇ )

I have had a few of these charming blue Wedgwood plates for years and rarely used them. ¬†So I was glad to be able to put them to use. ¬†The silver balls from Christmas still work well for New Year’s, and a few timepieces for the holiday have been added – including this pocket watch that belonged to my husband’s grandfather.

Everything looks better with candlelight.

Here is a little more of it tied to another silver ball and a tag on the piano… midnight kisses for New Year’s Eve. üôÇ

Now come with me into the dining room. ¬†All the red is gone for awhile. ¬†(You know it will return for Valentine’s Day.) ¬†I couldn’t totally abandon Christmas in there, so a wreath still hangs on the cabinet – but with black and blue this time.

Those number plates work great for New Year’s celebrations. (I wish I had bought many more years ago when they were in Target.)

And now to the table…

Like blogger Holly Mathis whose Christmas cards and tags were gorgeous this season (and always!), I tend to obsess over pretty paper…such as the line from Sugar Paper at Target. ¬†For our New Year’s table, the kraft paper desk calendar pages made great placemats. ¬†(Calendar..New Year’s.. get it? üôÇ )

I used the number plates here, too. ¬†The small buckets hold individual servings of sparkling cider (and a striped straw.)

Our “centerpiece” (if you can call it that) is a large footed bowl holding jars. ¬†Are you wondering why we need jars?

Well, my plan is for everyone to write down all the good things we have been blessed with this year on this form…

and the things we would like to do in 2014 on this form. (No pressure for resolutions…just dreams and goals for the new year.) ¬†We can then fold the papers, tuck them in the jars, and keep them to read again on our next New Year’s Day.

I also wrapped boxes of sparklers in white copy paper (yes, copy paper), hand lettered Happy New Year on the outside, and put them at each place setting.

This meal (Hoppin’ John, turnip greens, ambrosia, and cornbread) will be served buffet style from the kitchen, so we don’t have to worry about moving the jars and things from the table to serve the food.

Unfortunately, all of this won’t be enjoyed on New Year’s Eve. ¬†Originally, I had planned to do a party including our friend who broke his ankle last month, but the doctor has not given him the go-ahead to be up and about after his reconstruction surgery..so I will wait on him for another dinner party. ¬†Then¬†I had hoped to have the children here with some of their friends, but two of our three children have gone out of town for several days.

So hopefully we will enjoy it all as a belated dinner party later this week when everyone is here. ¬†I had thought about leaving the table set. ¬†(We can easily eat in the family room at the game table and be close to the fire in the fireplace.) ¬†But…my husband is currently running a fever, coughing, and he says that he feels achy all over. üôÅ I am crossing my fingers that it is not the flu, but it sure sounds like it might be. So I will get all the dishes out of the “range of germs” until he is well again.

Enough of all that. ¬†I am happily sitting by the fire tonight – enjoying this quiet evening (well, it was quiet until the neighbors started shooting off fireworks! üôÇ ) ¬†I hope you are celebrating the evening with those who are special to you. Thank you for making my experience in this world of blogging so amazing in 2013. ¬†You can’t imagine how many times I have gotten all tear-y eyed (in a good way!) and had a lump in my throat when I have read your comments and emails. ¬†Cry baby here loves them. ¬†I look forward to more fellowship with you in the coming year. ¬†Now that the rush of the season (and November’s photo shoot and yard sale) are behind us, I hope to get caught up on all your emails in the next few days (assuming I can uncover my desk beneath all the boxes from Christmas!) Wishing you good health, much humor, and a lot of love on this New Year’s Eve.

Happy New Year!
