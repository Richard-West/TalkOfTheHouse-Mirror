Weekend Wandering with Mason Jars and last chance for the Giveaway

print

This post is going to be like two posts in one because this day has gone a little off track here.

 Here is post #1 started earlier today.

Happy Weekend!  If you have looked at any of my table posts here, you know I am a fan of Mason jars.  Their uses are endless, and they bring a certain charm to any event.  So I thought this weekend we would wander through some photos I have rounded up showing the beloved mason jar.  First up is an arrangement that would work beautifully for autumn – especially here in the South with everyone harvesting cotton right now.

stylemepretty.com

There are so many uses of them with food.  How about red velvet cupcakes in a Mason jar?

stylemepretty.com 

Or perhaps you would prefer cheesecake in a jar? Not exactly a Mason jar, but the same concept.

mybakingaddiction.com

Several years ago, I was in New York City to attend workshops at Columbia University with some teacher friends of mine.  We did not get a lot of time to shop due to all the classes, but we did make time to eat!  My favorite memory of the entire trip was dinner one evening at Sarabeth’s Restaurant.

sarabethswest.com

sarabethswest.com

The dinner was the most traditional meal we had there in the city, and it felt like comfort food.  We dined outside on the sidewalk and chatted for hours there in the candlelight.  (I thought they might make us leave!)  One of the wonderful desserts at Sarabeth’s is their chocolate pudding – served in a jar.  And you get to keep the jar when you are finished!  It was truly a memorable evening.

source

Besides using them for food, Mason jars work beautifully for drinks, too.

stylemepretty.com

I thought these lights made from Mason jars were gorgeous!  How about hanging them out on your deck?

bourbonandboots.com

I know these cookies are not really Mason jars, but they were just too darn cute not to include here.karaspartyideas.com

With Christmas not too far away,  these do-it-yourself snowglobes are a perfect use for the jars.

whipperberry.com

Now I was supposed to write the end of the blog post here and ask you if you have some creative uses for Mason jars, but then something happened today, and I thought you might want to read about it.  So here is the second part of today’s post…

I don’t usually take many photographs outside, but today’s weather was so beautiful that I went outside early this morning and took these photos.

The trees here are really just starting to show their fall colors.

But this was the sight late this afternoon.

I had gone to throw out some trash, and the dog was barking strangely.  So I took a look in the direction he was barking, and that was when I saw the forest on fire.  I yelled for my husband, and we both tried to put it out with rakes and shovels but finally decided that we needed some help from these guys.

I appreciate our volunteer firemen for helping out this afternoon – when they could have been watching football in the comfort of their own homes.  I have only had one other time when I was glad for the sound of sirens – and that was when I was waiting for an ambulance for my father when he was having a heart attack. Today I was very glad when that truck pulled in and I could get out of the smoke.

It burned about an acre of the forest, but only one tree (a dogwood) seemed to really be harmed by the fire.  It was mostly low and burning the leaves…still scary though….and LOTS of smoke.  My husband and I are both doing a good job coughing this evening. Somewhere along the way, I pulled off my cardigan and threw it in the yard.  I have yet to find it…bet the dog got it!   Needless to say, my blogging plans for this afternoon did not get accomplished.

So that has been our Saturday.  Enough about the fire.  Let me get back to normal blogging here and say, tonight is the last chance to enter the drawing that will be held at midnight (since I am always up late) for the Williams Sonoma pumpkin cupcake mixes that will make you the star of Thanksgiving! ha ha. (You probably are already the star, right?)  Just leave a comment on one of the posts I have published since last week and you are entered.

So….do you have any creative uses for Mason jars??

I’d love to hear from you!
