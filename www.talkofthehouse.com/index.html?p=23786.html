Talk of the Celebration: A Bowling Birthday Party

print

I have one last celebration to share with you from our week of celebration-overload. üôÇ After 2 retirement parties, an evening overnight for Valentine’s Day followed by a delicious brunch at a local restaurant on Sunday, we came to my husband’s birthday the following day… February 15.

On our very first date back in the dark ages, he took me bowling. ¬†I was not very good at it, but he was. ¬†So good, in fact, he played on a bowling league during our first year of marriage.

This birthday party was a small one – a very casual dinner. ¬†So I thought a little bowling decor in a few places around the house would be an easy-peasy way to have a theme going on.

I found a bowling play set at Walmart – complete with 10 large pins and both a red and a black bowling ball.

It was perfect – except for one thing. ¬†The pins were plain white. ¬†So I used some Scotchguard brand red washi tape to make the “stripes” around them.

Self-adhesive Tapes 32.8 Ft Assorted 10m Scotch Expressions Washi Tape – C314 Red

It wasn’t perfect, but it worked well enough. I had to clip it several time to make the straight tape work on the curved surface of the pins.

For the dining room, I blew up balloons and put adhesive dots on them to mimic the holes in bowling balls. ¬†(I am sure you have seen all of these ideas on Pinterest. It was my go-to source for inspiration for this celebration.)

Pinterest even inspired the dots on the plastic plates (again from Walmart) to look like bowling balls and the stripes on the plastic forks and spoons (again with the washi tape) to mimic pins.

For this party, I only made the birthday cake. ¬†Everything else came from Mellow Mushroom – a Hey Dude pizza, a Caesar Salad pizza, their Garlic Butter and Parmesan pretzels, and my favorite – their bruschetta. ¬†(I apologize for the blurry photos. Blind girl here was in a hurry. When the food arrived everyone was ready to eat!)My husband requested a caramel cake for his birthday cake – definitely one of the most challenging ones for me. ¬†But it turned out really well, and I will do a post on the recipes for you soon. üôÇ

The cake was decorated with a bowling set I ordered from Amazon.
(Obviously our cake looked nothing like this!)

Bowling DecoSet Cake Decoration

After we ate, we all headed over to the bowling alley to play a game or two. ¬†When we arrived, I knew the parking lot seemed exceedingly crowded, but we managed to find a parking spot. ¬†I had hoped that Monday would not have been a popular night. ¬†Wrong. ¬†When we got inside, we learned there would not be an available lane for another 2 hours! ¬†Monday night is LEAGUE night. üôÅ ¬†So we sent our guests on their way with a rain check for another night to bowl, and we went to visit my husband’s parents instead (with slices of caramel cake for them. üôÇ )

And that was the last of our celebration overloaded week.

Things have settled down here some, but we do have a wedding to attend Saturday afternoon, and our daughter is one of the bridesmaids. ¬†üôÇ

Let the happy celebrations continue!

Until next time…
