Peach Ice Cream, a Little Progress, and Last Chance for the Giveaway

print

Happy Father’s Day to all the dads out there!  I hope today has been a relaxing and fun day for everyone. When you live in the same town as your parents and your in-laws, holidays are triple busy.  We began our day by celebrating the first Sunday for our church’s new pastor, and our son on crutches even made it up the stairs to the church balcony (and down again, too! 🙂 ) Then we enjoyed lunch at our local Sunday favorite restaurant, The Swanson.

They were packed today!  My fried chicken was great, and we left quite full.  But since it was Father’s Day we had to make ice cream.  Yes, I know. Again.  But there really were good reasons for it.  First, my father is a BIG fan of ice cream.  So when we make it,  I always call him to come over to have a serving (or two, or three…)  My father-in-law also likes it.  So it just seemed quite appropriate to make it for them on Father’s Day.  And another big reason to make it was this:

Remembering Len Berg’s Restaurant

I gave both my husband and my father a copy of this book today.  (Remember we talked about it here?) One of the things Len Berg’s was famous for was their homemade ice cream they served during the summer with fresh Georgia peaches. So I really wanted to try out their recipe for it.

Here’s the recipe if you would like to try it.

The reviews were mixed.  My daughter (the one who is on pain meds for having all her wisdom teeth removed Friday) said it was the best peach ice cream she had ever eaten – and she does not normally like peach ice cream.

My son and I liked it, but my father and my husband both thought it would be better if vanilla extract was used in place of the almond extract.  (The almond flavor was pretty strong.)  Next time we make it, we will try the vanilla instead.

In other news, we’ve made a little  progress in furniture around here.   We finally said good-bye to this piece:

It got a ride on a truck in the rain to the upholsterer’s and will be returning soon wearing khaki linen.  Boy have I been missing my favorite napping spot.

We have also purchased a table and chair set that I will be showing you very soon.  I have been shopping for several years for this and was super happy to finally find something that had everything I wanted in terms of style and durability.

And that’s all the news for this Father’s Day.  Because this post is going out so late and I want everyone to have a chance to leave a comment for one last entry in the Designer Dad autographed book giveaway, I am going to wait to close the entries until tomorrow night (6/17/13) at midnight.  Remember, all you need to do to be entered is leave a comment on this post or the two prior posts about Stephen Saint-Onge (one comment per post please.)  That means that you can enter 3 times for your chance at one of three copies of No Place Like Home.

No Place Like Home: Tips & techniques for real family-friendly home design

I started the posts on Stephen Saint-Onge, the Designer Dad, last week because I wanted to celebrate fathers.  I will close tonight by saying how lucky I am to be a part of families that have such wonderful dads.  As an educator, I often have students who do not have fathers in their lives.  Good fathers make such a huge difference, and I know being one is not an easy task.  So my love and respect goes out to all fathers on this Father’s Day.

And I will leave you with a talk about question…

What is/was your dad’s favorite food?

Hope to hear from you!
