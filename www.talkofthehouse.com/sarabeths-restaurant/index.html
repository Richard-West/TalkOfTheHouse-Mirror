Now let me give you a little background information on how Sarabeth’s restaurant began. Â Back in the early 80’s Sarabeth and her husband Bill Levine made jars of orange apricot marmalade in their home from an old family recipe. They sold it to local food suppliers there in New York City. Â It was a popular item, so they decided to open a small bakery in 1981 and sell preserves and baked goods. Â Here is the first Sarabeth’s Kitchen, where shoppers could not only buy the delicious items, but also watch as they were being prepared.

sarabeth.com

With the popularity of the baked goods and preserves, it made sense that they began to add seating to serve breakfast. Â Then they eventually expanded into serving lunch, and the popularity continued to grow. Â So a full restaurant was added on the Upper East side in 1983 – which included serving dinner. Â Brunch was added somewhere along the way.
