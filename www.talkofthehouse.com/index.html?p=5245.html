Weekend Wandering at Toad Hall

print

source

Happy weekend!  In our last two posts, we have had a taste of Blackberry Farm.  Let’s finish wandering there today.  I did not want to leave the Farm without giving you this last little bit of it – a dessert so to speak. 🙂

The founders of Blackberry Farm are Sandy and Kreis Beall, the parents of the current proprietor, Sam Beall.  Sandy is also the founder of the Ruby Tuesday’s line of restaurants, and Kreiss is a major lover of homes.  Together they have worked on over 40 houses – saving them, bringing them back to a beautiful life.  One of their residences is a compound adjacent to Blackberry Farm that they named Toad Hall.

architecturaldigest.com

With the development of private residences at Blackberry Farm, I am not sure if this is still their current home.  Regardless, it is a beautiful one to see.  It sits on 32 acres, and the five cabins in the compound cover 12,000 square feet.  The Bealls purchased the antique timbers from a collection accumulated by a man in Knoxville, Tennessee, and architect Jack Davis designed the homes.

architecturaldigest.com

Kreiss Beall has major decorating talent in her own right, but she collaborated with designer Suzanne Kasler on the interiors of Toad Hall.  The Beall’s extensive collection of rare 18th and 19th century European antiques are used throughout the residence giving it a wonderful mix of rough walls with refined furniture.  I really like the yellow mixed in the spaces to give it some lightness amid all of the dark.

suzannekasler.com

The kitchen is open to the great room.  The oak rack holding all that pewter over the gorgeous range is 19th century from England, and the French paintings are from the 18th century.

architecturaldigest.com

This master bedroom is just so dreamy. I have always wanted a fireplace in my bedroom (and one on the porch too.)  Isn’t the contrast between the pretty feminine florals and the rough masculine architecture perfect for a master bedroom?

architecturaldigest.com

This is another bedroom on the compound.  Again you have the roughness in the design of the hickory bench and four poster bed against the softness of the canopy and bed drapes.

I think the porch is one of the best spaces of Toad Hall.  With that view, why would you go anywhere else in the cabin? (Well, maybe to the kitchen for food.)

architecturaldigest.com

They have a fireplace on the porch too…sigh.hickoryconstruction.com

A small 2 room guest cottage, appropriately named Tadpole Cottage sits across the pond from the main house.  You can see it from the porch.

architecturaldigest.com

The Writer’s Cottage sits beyond the apple trees.  All of the landscaping was done by Mary Palmer Dargan, and she has planted English gardens everywhere on the property.  Just like at Blackberry Farm, there is great attention to detail here.

architectural digest.com

Jeff Herr did the photography for the feature on Toad Hall in Architectural Digest back in 2009.  He has a nice site that you might want to wander through also.  I hope the rest of your weekend is fun and that you have enjoyed wandering around all the areas of Blackberry Farm this week.

until next time…
