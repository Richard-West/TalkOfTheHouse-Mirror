For the Love of Red

source

You know I can’t resist the color red.  February has arrived full force in the shops with every imaginable kind of red item for Valentine’s Day.  It’s not quite as good as all the red at Christmas, but it’s close! (Plus all the chocolate candy doesn’t hurt either, does it?)

houseandhome.com

I think having something red in a room adds warmth and cheerfulness to the space, so my post today has rooms with red accents for some eye candy.  I know all of you have seen this next entrance a zillion times, but it is one red space that just says, Welcome home to me…love those doors!

nestofposies-blog.com

This library in a Martha’s Vineyard home featured in a very old House Beautiful article has been one I have adored forever.   The contrast of the red with all that white trim just makes that space so happy.  Be sure you click on the link to see the whole place (especially the mudroom-it’s gorgeous!)

housebeautiful.com

Here is another of my favorites.  This one is from an old Better Homes and Gardens article about a 1912 beach house.  Again, check out the link for the whole cute cottage!  The rooms in it have small doses of red, but they work perfectly against all the light upholstery in the room.

bhg.com

The two red settees in this family room are a big dose of color, and the pillows on the sofa carry that red through to the other side of the room.

billybesonco.com

Let’s jump over to bedrooms with some red now.  The quilt in this Sarah Richardson farmhouse room work beautifully with the paisley and polka dot pillows.  Love the mix!  (Wish I could be that brave.)

nestofposies-blog.com

This next bedroom is a little more subtle in its use of red.  Yes, there is a red bed, but also notice there is red trim on the edge of the drapes, and there is a red model airplane suspended in the corner.

stringerinc.com

Here is a precious window seat in another child’s bedroom – this one from a shingled home on Long Island.

traditionalhome.com

Now to the kitchens…Don’t you just love those Windsor bar stools with that red print rug?!

countryliving.com

This has got to be one of the tallest cabinets I think I have ever seen in a kitchen – major use of red going on there!

bhg.com

The most frequent use of red in kitchens seems to be on the stools – especially in kitchens with white cabinets (and I have certainly entertained the idea of  painting both our stools and dining room chairs red!)

houzz.com

houzz.

And finally, I think the red floors in this kitchen are simply wonderful!

stagetecture.com

In this month of love, I love many things…obviously red being one of them.  After writing five Loving Mitford posts Jan Karon’s books are another (read more about them here,) and I also love my readers!  So who won the Mitford giveaway??

 Here is the winning number:

That comment belonged to none other than Sarah from House-Crazy Sarah on the post about Jan Karon’s homes.

Congratulations Sarah!  I will be in touch with you about getting your Mitford things to you soon.  I clicked on Sarah’s site tonight to see what wonderful house stuff she has going on, and my eyes got all teary-fied.  You see, her post is on another of my loves – John Denver.

His was the very first concert I remember attending as a teenager. (Yes, I am that old.)  And boy did I ever have a major crush on him! I know my family had to get sick of hearing Country Roads, Rocky Mountain High, and every other piece of music he ever performed. He died way too young.  You can read about what has become of his house at Sarah’s post.

And do you know what else is even more crazy about all of this? Sarah is from Colorado (and John Denver lived there.)  No one in our family has ever been to Colorado, but my middle child just left this week for an “adventure” (his words – not mine.)  He is traveling with his camping gear in his back pack for a month to see the West, and he is right this minute in…. you guessed it… Denver, Colorado!  How crazy is that??

I’ll leave you with one more “love of red” photo.  Don’t shoot me…it’s a Christmas one (I just couldn’t resist!)  This little cottage is the epitome of red’s ability to bring a touch of cheer and celebration to whatever it touches. 🙂

highstreetmarket.blogspot.com

So..do you like red?
