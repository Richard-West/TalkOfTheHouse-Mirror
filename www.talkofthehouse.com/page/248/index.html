Black and White in Our House

The other day we looked at some houses and rooms that showcased the use of black and white.  You already know I love the color scheme.  So today I thought we would look at how it recurs around here.  The most obvious place it appears is in the kitchen and dining area.

I really have a fondness for red, but after the overdose of it during the Christmas holidays, I like a clean slate in January.  So some of the red is put away for a few weeks until it reappears for February’s day of love.  The black and white seems fresh to me after all the holiday decorations.

The dining room table is set with my plain white dishes again. (Farewell Christmas china….)  Plain white cloth napkins are tied with black grosgrain ribbon, and the centerpiece is camellias (cut from the yard) loosely arranged in a white soup tureen.  Such a fresh contrast to the gingerbread houses and the patterned red napkins of just a month ago.

Oreos and sugar cookies fill the cookie jars on the buffet, and dessert plates and napkins get a fair share of black and white here, too.

Black and white bound cookbooks take the place of the red ones in the cabinet in there.  No dab of red is left.  (Don’t get me wrong.  I do love Christmas.  I just need a break from some of the red for a little while.)

Another place where I’ve used black and white is the children’s bathroom.  I don’t think I have shown you that room yet.  Have I?

The chalkboard is the ultimate place for black and white. (Ignore the ghost letters from Christmas behind the words.)

The master bath has a touch of the color scheme in its simple styling.

All the red has also been removed from the foyer with some black and white taking its place.

And of course you have already seen all the black and white in the playroom…

and in the boys’ room.

I’m not going to bore you with the black and white check kitchen curtains since you have seen them enough already. 🙂  But I will show you the clothing I have added to my wardrobe in the last couple of weeks.

talbots.com

Yep, I love black and white.  So do you think there is enough black and white around here?

Don’t worry.  I haven’t abandoned all the other colors.

I also purchased these last week…

 

talbots.com

Red will be making its comeback in about a week with the arrival of February. Until then I will enjoy the classic look of the black and white around here.

Hope you enjoyed it, too.

A Love for Black and White

source

I was looking through the houses that I have pinned on my Pinterest boards and noticed that I had a trend going on ….white houses with some kind of  black accent – either shutters, the front door, or the roof. And it wasn’t just once.  It was over and over again. (Can you believe that house above is modular?)

idyllexplorer.com

Do you find that there is often a trend in the things that you are drawn to –  something that recurs over and over again?

timbarberltd.com

I think that is one really beneficial thing about Pinterest.  It allows you to accumulate images and then see similarities between them that you might not have otherwise noticed.  If you are building a home or renovating a space, this would be very helpful.  Not only did I notice that in my Exteriors board there were numerous white houses…

catalystarch.com

but in some of my other boards the use of black and white showed up more than once.  I thought this next one had an unusual combination (but still black and white.)

herlongarchitects.com

And the combination continued in these…just a little differently.  How about a black house with white trim?

vineetkaur.tumblr.com

lambandblonde.blogspot.com

This is my favorite of them.houseandhome.com

Then of course there are the interiors with all their black and white goodness.  I have loved this kitchen with its unique ceiling treatment for a long time… and the fact that it is in Nantucket doesn’t hurt either. 🙂

traditionalhome.com

And … I have thought about painting my island black as a contrast to the other cabinetry in the kitchen but have not gotten quite brave enough to do it yet.

bhg.com

This dining “room” is in a very small home where they utilized the wide space of the entry hall.  I like how the area looks elegant and simple at the same time thanks to the use of few accessories plus the classic look of the black and white together.

countryliving.com

And then of course, there is my addiction to black and white checked fabric (kind of obvious if you have looked at the rooms in my house, isn’t it?)  The drapes in this room would fit right in here.

compulsivelycompiled.blogspot.com

I would love to have this chair for the room we are trying to finish over our garage.

stillstars.com

And last, but certainly not least, here is a room that is not afraid to use every drop of black and white loveliness that it can find!  Too much for you?

source

Yes, I am hopelessly addicted to black and white.  Do you have a color or perhaps an accessory addiction that keeps showing up in your home (or on your Pinterest boards?)

We’d love to hear about it!
