Gone to the Dogs

source

I must admit I am not a University of Georgia bulldog fan.  I have been a Ga. Tech fan for as long as I can remember.  My brother and a dear friend of mine graduated from Tech, and I had a scholarship offer to attend there, but I do see the attraction to UGA.  My daughter spent this past Saturday afternoon “between the hedges” at her first UGA game with some friends.  This was her view in the sea of red.

I watched the game on television in between working on our sons’ bedroom, and oh my goodness what a game it was!!  It was a battle back and forth between Georgia and LSU all the way to the very end – with UGA winning 44 – 41…quite an exciting one to watch.  Even when watching football, you know my brain strays into the land of home design.  I thought it would be fun to take a look at some doggie decor today…starting with this cutie.

potterybarn.com

This coin bank from Pottery Barn would make a nice Christmas gift for a UGA fan, wouldn’t it? Pottery Barn also has these pillows that can be personalized with your pup’s name.

potterybarn.com

And I loved these St. Bernard appetizer plates they offered in their latest catalog.

potterybarn.com

They used to sale this great dog cubbie, but unfortunately it is not available anymore.

petsugar.com

Pottery Barn is not the only one to have dog themed items in their stores, though.  Take a look at this child’s bedroom from an old Restoration hardware’s RH Baby line catalog.

The dog artwork is no longer available, but those precious sheets can still be purchased.

rhbabyandchild.com

At one time they also had these great dog bookends.

source

Now let’s take a look at a few rooms that have a dog theme going on in them.  Isn’t that large piece of art in this child’s room wonderful?

mydesigndump.blogspot.com

Here is another bedroom for you to see.  Check out all the art and the throw pillow on the bed.

houzz.com

I have seen this wallpaper used in a number of rooms lately, but I really like how they added the accents of yellow in this particular space.

oharainteriors.com

We have looked at this next room way back when I did a post on entryways, but the dog artwork in it is too good not to include it here.

bhg.com

Not only do we find dog themes in room decor, but they are also popping up in children’s birthday parties everywhere.  Here is a peek at one of my favorite “dog parties.” (And there are even free printables on that site if you will click on the link below.)

onecharmingparty.com

You know I am a big fan of Susan Branch.  Did you know that she has a precious set of doggie cookie cutters available for sale on her site?  You could use them to make treats for your own puppy party! 🙂

susanbranch.com

And she has a beautifully illustrated recipe for real dog treats on her website, here. (It is near the bottom of that post, so be sure you go far enough down to find it.)

That brings us to the end of our post today.  I hope you have enjoyed seeing some of the many dog themed items and rooms that are out there.  As I have worked on our sons’ room, I contemplated using dog accessories in there but went in a different direction instead.  Fingers crossed I will be able  to show you some updated photos of that room this week.

onecharmingparty.com

And I will leave you with another question to talk about:

Do you have pets?  If so, what are they?  (We are definitely dog folks in our house.)

Looking forward to hearing from you!

September 25, 2013

Sarabeth’s Restaurant

source

We’ve been all over New York City in my last two posts, but I have one last place I want to share with you today.  And no, it has nothing to do with the movie You’ve Got Mail this time.  Sorry – but this is a wonderful place and would have made for a great scene in the movie, too.  There is a bit of information for a different funny movie at the end of the post for you, though…so hang on. 🙂

I had done my research and found that if you want an outstanding brunch in NYC, go to one of the Sarabeth’s restaurants.  That would be the Sarabeth of the marmalade and jam maker fame…

williams-sonoma.com

Unfortunately we never had time to enjoy a lazy morning brunch while we were there.  (It was grab what you want at Starbuck’s and then get on the subway each morning.)  But…I had read that one of the best kept secrets in New York City was Sarabeth’s serves a great dinner, too…and their Upper West side location was right around the corner from our hotel.  So on our last night in New York City we walked down the block to this precious place…

nymag.com

I cannot even begin to tell you the feeling of coming home that we all felt when we walked into that restaurant.  Keep in mind that we were four Southern small town girls who had been in the Big Apple for awhile.  We had been to deli’s, classes at Columbia University, Rockefeller Center, shopping on Columbus Circle, seen Wicked on Broadway, road a ferry boat out in NY harbor at night…all wonderful, but very different from our lifestyle at home.  Sarabeth’s, for some reason felt…familiar….almost Southern in nature.  The dining room reminded us of the dining room in an old hotel in our home town.

nymag.com

But in all our dining in NYC, we had not had the experience of eating at a sidewalk cafe, and the weather that particular evening was wonderfully cool, so, as pretty as the dining room was, we opted to be seated at one of their sidewalk tables.  And what an enjoyable evening we had!  We ate and talked, and ate and talked some more, for almost 2 1/2 hours.  That memory remains in my mind as one of the best dining experiences I have had in my life…the company, the conversation, the food, the atmosphere.

We were ready for some comfort food at that point in the trip, and Sarabeth’s did not disappoint. I believe I had a burger and fries,

sarabethsrestaurants.com

and one of my friends had the chicken pot pie.

sarabethsrestaurants.com

I don’t recall the other two dishes, but I do remember the desserts.  (Afterall, if you are eating at Sarabeth’s you have to order dessert. :)) I had the cookie plate which had a sampling of five of their cookies.

source

Someone ordered the strawberry shortcake.

goddessofbakedom.com

And best of all was the chocolate pudding served in jars.

dnainfo.com

Now let me give you a little background information on how Sarabeth’s restaurant began.  Back in the early 80’s Sarabeth and her husband Bill Levine made jars of orange apricot marmalade in their home from an old family recipe. They sold it to local food suppliers there in New York City.  It was a popular item, so they decided to open a small bakery in 1981 and sell preserves and baked goods.  Here is the first Sarabeth’s Kitchen, where shoppers could not only buy the delicious items, but also watch as they were being prepared.

sarabeth.com

With the popularity of the baked goods and preserves, it made sense that they began to add seating to serve breakfast.  Then they eventually expanded into serving lunch, and the popularity continued to grow.  So a full restaurant was added on the Upper East side in 1983 – which included serving dinner.  Brunch was added somewhere along the way.

sarabethsrestaurants.com

Then the restaurant on the Upper West side where we dined opened in 1986.  In 1998 Sarabeth’s at Chelsea Market opened. (I think we would all have a great time eating our way through Chelsea Market!)

themarthablog.com

Two Sarabeth’s restaurants opened in 2005.  One in Central Park South…

sarabethsrestaurants.com

and the other in….Key West! (Not sure how that one happened.)

mycookingmagazine.com

In 2011, a Sarabeth’s restaurant opened in the Tribeca district of New York City.

downtownmagazinenyc.com

downtownmagazinenyc.com

Another Sarabeth’s restaurant opened in Tokyo in November of 2012 (with another scheduled there for later this year.)  Apparently the brunch craze is going strong in Japan.

tripadvisor.co.uk

And finally, one more opened in New York City on Park Avenue South earlier this year. It has a much more sophisticated feel – quite different from the charming traditional Upper West side location.

facebook.com

So as you can see, there are quite a few Sarabeth’s restaurants in NYC (and I haven’t even shown you all of the bakeries.)  But not everyone can travel to New York (or Key West or Tokyo) to enjoy the food from Sarabeth.  I know you have probably seen the jams and jellies in stores.  Our nearby Fresh Market carries them.  But did you know you can order the baked items and other goodies online?

How about a breakfast in bed gift package?

sarabeth.com

or a loaf cake trio?

sarabeth.com

Or perhaps you would like half a dozen of their little ginger pecan tarts. Don’t they look yummy?

surlatable.com

The tomato soup at the restaurants is supposed to be outstanding.  I wish one of us had ordered it.  It is even available for ordering frozen online.

sarabeth.com

But if you don’t want to do that, you can whip up Sarabeth’s dishes in your own home with her excellent cookbook.  It is the most professional cookbook that I have in my collection with pages of information to teach you baking techniques – in a totally understandable step by step style.

Sarabeth’s Bakery: From My Hands to Yours

If you would like to see an interview with Sarabeth herself and learn how she makes biscuits, just click on the video link below.

foxnews.com

And finally, I found something I thought was so very interesting.  I promised you a bit of movie information, right?   How about this…

Many of you mentioned It’s Complicated as one of your favorite movies.  Well, did you know the bakery kitchen scenes in the movie were filmed in Sarabeth’s Chelsea Market Bakery?

source

And guess who trained Meryl Streep on baking techniques for the movie?  Yep, none other than Sarabeth Levine.  And not only that.  She was also Meryl’s hand double in the movie!  Love it.

facebook.com

And I’m afraid that brings us to the end of our New York City trip.  Thank you so much for coming along with me!  After looking at all the food photos in this post, I am hungry!  How about you? I am going to leave you with a foodie question today:

What is your favorite made from scratch food item to bake?

Looking forward to hearing from you!
