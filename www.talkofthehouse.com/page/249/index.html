January 12, 2013

My Favorites – Phoebe Howard Part 3

source

It’s Saturday.  I know many of you are looking out your window and seeing snow.  Don’t hate me.  It will be 80 degrees here today (and it is sunny)- yes, in the middle of January.  In this final post of Phoebe Howard rooms, I wanted to share my favorites with you.

phoebehoward.net

It seems that many of my favorite rooms are in vacation homes – or they are at least in locations that many people go for a vacation.  So I want to help you escape the cold and go on a virtual vacation here today. Wouldn’t it be great to be sitting on one of these porches?

phoebehoward.net

The one at the very top of the post is in Atlantic Beach, Florida.  The two shown here with that lovely shade of blue green are from the 2011 Coastal Living Idea house Phoebe Howard decorated in the East Beach development of Norfolk, Virginia, and this is the cute stairway from that same Idea House.

phoebehoward.net

Now let’s travel from East Beach up to East Hampton to the Howards’ personal home.  I love this guest bedroom with its old flag on the wall.  It seems perfect in this location.

phoebehoward.net

And one day I want to make a headboard using a quilt like they did for this room.  Isn’t that gorgeous?!

phoebehoward.net

The kitchen in their home has a contrast of painted and stained wood cabinetry, and glass doors are in the mix, too…all my favorites.

phoebehoward.net

Let’s go back south now to another beach location – Sea Island.  Here is the kitchen Mrs. Howard has done there with very similar elements – white painted cabinets, some with glass front doors, mixed with a stained wood island.

mrshowardpersonalshopper.com

Now I am going to take you in a different direction on our little virtual vacation here.  We are going to leave the beaches and head to the mountains.  My all time favorite home that Phoebe Howard has done is this one in Cashiers, North Carolina (not far from Highlands).

housebeautiful.com

It has been featured in House Beautiful magazine and pinned over and over on Pinterest.  So you have probably seen it, but I can’t leave this home out if I am doing a post on my Phoebe Howard favorites…and this next room is my favorite of the favorites.

housebeautiful.com

The colors, the prints, the plank walls, the big window with the long simple drapes, and the mantle…!  I could easily plop down on that sofa and feel right at home.  Couldn’t you?  It makes me want to change the arrangement of the furniture in my great room, and the repeat of the simple accessories on the bookshelves makes me want to edit edit edit ours.

cotedetexas.blogspot.com

Now take a look at this staircase with its plank walls…the windows…the gallery of framed prints, and look up as you enter it.  The ceiling has painted planks, too.  Simply gorgeous.

phoebehoward.net

This is the bedroom hallway.  I am not normally a fan of antlers, but it does work here…and they are a common theme mixed with transferware in the Highlands/Cashiers area of North Carolina.

housebeautiful.com

The plank walls and ceilings continue in the master bedroom with its soft bed linens and drapes.  This room really has a cocoon like feel…something that would be needed in the cold weather in that mountain region.

housebeautiful.com

One of the guest rooms sports my favorite fabric for its drapes – buffalo checks.

housebeautiful.com

And finally, we will end like we began… with a porch.  I have shared these two photos on another post about porches, but since they belong to this wonderful house done by Phoebe Howard, I am sharing them again. (Forgive me for the rerun.)  This porch is definitely one of my favorites with its wicker furniture, neutral colors, and amazing view of the Appalachian Mountains.

phoebehoward.net

That brings us to the end of the Phoebe Howard mini-series and the end of our virtual vacation today.  I hope you have enjoyed the range of rooms that Mrs. Howard has done, and if you are snowed in, I hope this little “trip” has brought you some virtual sunshine. 🙂

phoebehoward.net

I swear it looks like late spring here with the sunshine, light breeze, and warm temps.  I just heard the screen door slam as my son headed outside to play basketball, and I am sitting here in the dining room with the doors wide open.  Crazy for the middle of January!

Enjoy your weekend.

The Joy of Decorating: Southern Style with Mrs. Howard

Phoebe Howard – Part 2

source

The other day we began by talking about Downton Abbey’s formal and fancy rooms, and somehow we moved into discussing Phoebe Howard’s dining rooms…not exactly related topics.  Today I thought we would take a look in some of Mrs. Howard’s lovely bedrooms.   Some are very extravagant with a huge canopy bed like this one….

mrshowardpersonalshopper.com

Some are quite impressive with their high ceilings, fine furnishings, and thoughtfully chosen linens.  Here are two views of one of those rooms.

houseofturquoise.com

goodhousekeeping.com

Or how about this one with its gorgeous rustic wood ceiling?source

mrshowardpersonalshopper.com

The subtitle of Phoebe Howard’s book is “Southern Style with Mrs. Howard.”  I don’t know if it is just me, but these rooms do seem to have a Southern feel…especially this next bedroom.

phoebehoward.net

What do you think?  Southern feel or not?  Did you know that she did not have any formal training in design?  To me, that means she has natural talent.   Her aunt from Montgomery, Alabama played a big role in Mrs. Howard’s life.  You will have to read the book to find out exactly what her influence was, but let’s just say Southern style runs in her family. 🙂

atlantahomesmag.com

When you describe many of Phoebe Howard’s bedrooms, the words warm, soft, and pretty come to mind.

This next room is a good example of that; don’t you think?

goodhousekeeping.com

cotedetexas.blogspot.com

And did you know that Mr. Howard is very important in their design business, too?  He is her business partner and is responsible for planning the space and creating the architectural elements that are such an important part of a room.  Each project is truly a reflection of the creativity and talent that they both possess.

phoebehoward.net

I have saved my favorite Phoebe Howard rooms for my final post on her designs. And I will tell you that they are not her formal spaces.  I hope you will be back next time to enjoy them!

See you then…

Rose L - January 10, 2013 - 2:37 pm

This set of pictures for the most part are much more comfortable to my eye than the last set. I do like them very much. I have looked through them so many times that I am glad that more than one person can view them at the same time on the internet because I would have otherwise caused quite a traffic jam. Ha!

Anyway, my analysis of what makes these rooms work is a little different from what some others have pointed out. What I think is note worthy is that as you look through them you might notice that when the ceilings are fancy, coiffured, beamed, etc. The walls wear a solid paint, always light in color. When the ceilings are unadorned other than for having crown molding the walls are papered with a mild textured wallpaper, and some with an artists feel to them but still light with muted colors. In one room with the very curvacious ceiling treatment it was so fancy that any other bed would have seemed way too plain so the canopy had to be MORE fancy than even the ceiling in order to stand out in the room. With all of that movement going on at ceiling height it was important that other elements had to be just as curvacious closer to floor level in order to even be noticed in the room (check out the bench and chair) and yet there is smooth white furniture in the room that gives the eye a place to rest. That keeps the room from becoming dizzying with all the pattern and texture.

The “vanilla” room is a little different because the walls and ceiling are plain but the bed more than makes up for the plainness of the room by adding plenty of texture to pull everything together. This is where Mrs. Howard’s work excels for me. She has an impeccable way of blending textures so nothing is too plain and nothing is too fancy where it overwhelms all of the other elements of the room. You might also notice that where the rooms are all light in color, most of the furniture pieces are in very dark tones to keep everything from being too light where it might all blend together without a feeling of any texture and pattern at all.

Figuring out what makes her rooms work makes me feel very confident in some of the work I’ve recently done in my own home. What is weird is that the room I just finished seems to totally work but but I could not figure out why it works so well. Now I can look at it through Mrs. Howard’s eyes and see that the ceiling is very textured, the walls are plain paint but in the softest Robin’s egg blue I could find and the furniture pieces are dark enough wood tones to keep it from looking like a cloud.
Thanks for this post Kelly, it has helped me learn some things that I will continue to apply to my own work to continue to get that soft but welcome feeling in the rest of the house as I move from room to room putting everything together. Now I wish I could find a loved designer who can teach me how to feel more confident in blending color between rooms so the change is subtle but not the same in every room. And, everything isn’t white to make it all blend, haha! Can a blog comment be too long? Sorry about that!

I love your comment about keeping it from “looking like a cloud.” That is why so many of the very light rooms I see these days do not appeal to me. Your room sounds lovely.
