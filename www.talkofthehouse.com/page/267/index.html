October 6, 2012

Weekend Wandering…in Yellow

source

Happy weekend!   I thought we would wander through some yellow on this fall weekend as I contemplate using more of it in my sons’ room – or maybe my daughter’s.  Ever since I saw the Blackberry Farm bedroom above, I have been in the mood for yellow. Are you a fan of the color?  I used to think of it mostly for spring, but if it goes in the direction of gold, it really warms up a space.  Let’s start by wandering in another bedroom with those great painted plank walls.

hgtv.com

Are you familiar with Randy Florke’s work?  He used to be an editor at Country Living magazine.  He is also into real estate and has written a couple of books on reusing furniture, accessories,and building materials.   His family’s home in a 1928 apartment in New York uses an abundance of yellow.

countryliving.com

countryliving.com

countryliving.com

The next is not Randy Florke’s work.  This pantry was done by Plain and Fancy cabinetry.  Isn’t it a gorgeous little space?!  I really need to find the rest of the kitchen that this pantry came from.  I bet it is beautiful.

oldhouseonline.com

Not only am I wanting to add more yellow to my house, but I am also wanting to add it to my wardrobe.  I love all the things pictured below – the sweater, the shoes, and the bag.  Doesn’t this look perfect for fall? Just put it with a pair of jean, and you are ready to go practically anywhere.

talbots.com

I thought this table setting with the yellow autumn elements was really pretty.  We need some leaves like that around here!

theswenglishhome.blogspot.dk

To wrap up our weekend wandering, here is a recipe for a weekend treat…crockpot boiled peanuts!  I always make them on the stove, but a crockpot would also work.  I hope you will make them, and then let me know how they turn out for you.

myrecipes.com

So add some yellow to your life, make some boiled peanuts, and enjoy your weekend!

Twenty Things to Love About Fall

source

Everyone seems to have done their fall favorites post, and the autumn decorating articles are out in full force.  So I am late to the party.  I promise I am thinking about it too, but the seasons are fickle around here.  At first we think that summer has disappeared and fall has arrived, but no…summer comes back again and again like an unwanted houseguest.  I should know by now that we can’t really count on autumn to stay until about Thanksgiving. Truly.  (How do people in Florida deal with this??)

themarionhousebook.com 

It makes it hard for me to put out the autumn decor.   So let me just pretend that the temperatures are cool outside and think about all the things I love in the season to come…maybe that will get me going on the fall decorating.  Anyone else suffer these same symptoms?  Here is a look at my fall loves…hope this helps in the inspiration department (especially for me… :))

1.  The sound of a fire crackling in our fireplace

2.  The sound of cheering crowds and marching bands at football games

en.wikipedia.org

(I even love the sound of them on television!)

3.  The smell of autumn scented candles

(My spiked cider scented rewined candle from Two Friends smells heavenly!)

4. The smell of woodsmoke from an outdoor firethebeehiveblog.net

5.  The snug feeling of extra blankets on the bedoctober-glory.tumblr.com

6.  The warmth of sweaters (since I am so cold natured!)habituallychic.blogspot.ca

7.  The coziness of a throw on a sofa or chaircountryliving.com

8.  The deliciousness of pumpkin cheesecake from Cheesecake Factory

(only available in the fall!)buzzfeed.com

9.  The sweetly spiced flavor of hot mulled cidermarthastewart.com

10.  All the chocolate goodness of Halloween candy that is out in the stores everywherethemeparkinsider.com

11.  The spicy taste of hot homemade chilitasteofhome.com

12.  Our yummy apple cake

13.  The salty taste of warm boiled peanutsmyrecipes.com

14.  The ooey-gooey-ness of roasted marshmallowssource

15.  The cheerfulness of happily colored mums

16.  The beautiful colors of the leaves in the north Georgia mountainsactiverain.com

17.  The bright colors of pumpkins everywhere you look24.media.tumblr.com

18.  Clever Halloween costumeskeikolynn.com

19.  Wonderful fall table settingstwigand thistle.com

and #20 – don’t hate me – Christmas decorations arriving in the stores!flickr.com

That wraps up my 20 favorite things about fall.  I bet you have many of the same loves.  What is your favorite thing about the season?

I always love to hear from you!

You’ve got a lot of reading to do then Linda! I don’t like cords either. My cords are well hidden. In fact when we were redoing my workroom and building a new cabinet, the outlet was not in the right place for me. I knew where I wanted my lamp on it, and I hate a “strung across” cord, so I had my husband cut a hole in the counter and place an outlet inside the cabinet.

The cord for the computer and lamp on my desk, run down the side of my desk, and the outlet is behind the drapes. The lamp on the end table by the sofa runs down the table, under the sofa, and to an outlet in the floor that we had put in when we built and I knew it was needed there. So I don’t really have a trick. I think I learned that I needed more outlets in the first house we built, so when we were putting in the electrical here, I drew out the floor plan with furniture arrangements on it, and knew where they would be needed in this house. I would still like a hole cut in our island for the lamp there since that cord snakes around the jars of cereal. Oh wait..there is one trick. In our bedroom on the bedside table, the cords to the lamp and phone and clock are taped to the back of the table and down the leg to the floor so that they do not hang visible behind it. 🙂

Okay the photos…yes, they work in the kitchen. I am only really messy over by the stove and cookbooks. There are no photos over on that wall except for one, and if it gets splattered, it is very easy to wipe that glass with a paper towel and Windex. (I also have to wipe those glass jars and the cookbook spines in that area from time to time.) The photos by the coffee trays never seem to get splattered at all. The biggest messy area is right behind the stove. It really needs a piece of stainless steel there to make it easier to clean, and there are no photos in that area.

Hope that helps to answer your questions. Thank you for reading the blog!!
Kelly
