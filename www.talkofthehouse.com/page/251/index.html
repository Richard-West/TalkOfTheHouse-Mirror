The Best of 2012

The Christmas holidays have flown past, and I find it hard to believe it is already New Year’s Eve.  I did not get a chance to show you the great room in the Christmas posts…in fact, I totally forgot to photograph it! But the picture above from last year looks identical to this year’s decorating.  I did take pictures on Christmas morning, though.

Here is the before.  Santa’s gifts were waiting for the sleeping kids to get up.

During the happy chaos (One got a Nook, and one got a Kindle.  We’re definitely readers around here.)

And after all the gift giving was done

While my oldest was home for Christmas I did manage to coerce all three into finally taking a photograph for next year’s Christmas cards (since we could not get one done this year.)

And we had a couple of silly pictures when we tried to get the dog in on the action.

I sadly started taking down all the Christmas decorations yesterday – with Christmas music playing. 🙂  The putting away continued today (and will for the next several days.)  My husband worked on the outside, and he has worked on taking the ornaments and lights off the Christmas tree all afternoon while watching the Georgia Tech game. (They won!)  This is how it looked before he began to work on it.

(picture of tree from last year, but it looked the same this year)

Like everyone else seems to be doing, I want to take a look back at 2012 before it is totally written in the history books.  My #1 New Year’s Resolution last year was to start a blog, and it has been such a blessing to have been able to do this.  I have been so lucky to have been able to share all of this with you this year and to have gotten to “know” so many of you through your comments and emails. Thank YOU for this!

In school, I was always a “math kid,” so I can’t help but look at numbers.  I thought I would share the top 5 of some things from 2012 from Talk of the House. So here goes…

From all of these numbers, I think it is safe to conclude that you all like the kitchen and eating!  (Not bad things in my mind!)

I also want to thank my top 5 referring sites from this year.  They have helped me soooooo much to get my blog out there and into the eyes of readers.

So Happy New Year to all of you.  I have had a very blessed year, and my wish for you is that 2013 will be filled with days full of good things with those you love.  I am looking forward to sharing the new year with you!

Christmas in Vermont…One Day

source

It has been awhile since we have looked at an inn here.  We have been stuck on my house for over a month now, so let’s travel today.  The picture below is pinned to my “On My Bucket List” board on Pinterest.

gentlegiantsrides.com

One year I want to go on a ride in a “one horse open sleigh” at Christmas – preferably in Vermont.  I am not sure why I want to do this so badly.  Being as cold natured as I am, I will probably be freezing the whole time!  I guess it just looks romantic.  Did you know that some of the places that offer sleigh rides put wheels on their sleighs so that the horses can still pull them even if there is not a lot of snow?  I thought that was pretty smart.  And is there a difference in a sleigh and a sled?  Just wondering….

livelyslivery.com

Anyway, I have talked for years about spending Christmas in Vermont.  A week ago my middle child walked into the kitchen and asked, “Hey, why don’t we go to Vermont for Christmas?” (I must have brainwashed him.)  I told him that was a wonderful idea, but he should have asked that weeks ago when I started buying gifts and decorating for the holidays and when you could still find an available room in an inn.  If we go to Vermont for Christmas, the trip would be Christmas – meaning no other presents than the trip itself.  Needless to say, he didn’t ask again, but it did get me looking for a place to stay in Vermont if we ever make that trip.

And I did find what looks to be a wonderful place to stay.  See what you think.hermitageinn.com

It is the Hermitage Inn in West Dover, Vermont located at the base of Mount Snow. (Great name!) Doesn’t it look beautiful?  It even has a real covered bridge at its entrance.

2plus2nycreview.com

The Hermitage is a “Green Hotel” with everything pretty much being solar powered. You can read all about their green initiatives here – they are major.  But I know you want to see their rooms, right?  There are 15 rooms spread between their 3 buildings – the Main House, the Wine Wing, and their Carriage House.   Here are a few for you to choose from.

hermitageinn.com

selectregistry.com

greenspot.travel

hermitageinn.com

There are antiques, fireplaces, and flat screen televisions in the rooms.  The building was once a working farm, but its mission now is chiefly relaxation and enjoyment.  It has 2 hot tubs, a fitness center, a spa, and nightly bonfires.  You can choose from on site ice skating, snowmobiling, and cross country skiing as outdoor activities – not to mention the snow skiing at nearby Mount Snow.

And the food there has also been rated as very good.  I love the look of these skewers –  tomatoes, fresh mozzarella with herbs and a balsamic reduction.  A friend of mine made something very similar for the Christmas party we recently had here, and they were delicious!

hermitageinn.com

Here is a sample breakfast menu.hermitageinn.com

And here is their cozy dining room.selectregistry.com

So what do you think?  Does it look like a good place to spend Christmas?  My husband and children would love to be skiing.  I would love to be looking out at this view.

hermitageinn.com

but then again…

We could just watch a movie about being in Vermont for Christmas. 🙂

White Christmas [Blu-ray]

When I was a teenager I traveled to Williamsburg, Virginia during the Christmas holidays. It was beautifully decorated, and they were even predicting snow while we were there. But for some reason, I just wanted to be home for Christmas.  I am afraid that is what would happen if we travel during that time.  So perhaps it would be smarter to make the trip to Vermont right after Christmas.  Have any of you been away on a vacation for Christmas Day.  If so, did you want to be home, or were you perfectly happy to be where you were?

I’d love to hear from you. 🙂
