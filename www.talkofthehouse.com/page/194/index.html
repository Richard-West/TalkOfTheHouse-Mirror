Entertaining for the Season [and a Williams Sonoma book giveaway!]

Before I get into today’s post let me first say Happy Veterans Day to all of you and a big thank you to all the veterans and their families who give so much for our country.  I cannot even begin to imagine the sacrifices that have to be made by those who serve.

November 11…we are almost halfway through the month of November, and I know you are probably thinking about all the entertaining and decorating that will need to be done in the coming weeks.  As I think about it, I know there are tons of ideas on Pinterest, blogs, and other websites, but for me (and my bad eyesight!) nothing beats a book full of ideas that I can hold in my hands.  So with that in mind, I wanted to give away a copy of two of my favorite books for the season (and no I have no affiliation with Williams Sonoma – this is just from me. :))

I found this Thanksgiving book years ago. It was full of beautiful photographs and delicious recipes.

 Williams-Sonoma Entertaining: Thanksgiving Entertaining

It is organized around regional Thanksgiving dinners like this New England style one.

The book gives menus, recipes, directions on making the centerpieces and other table decorations, and a work plan for getting everything done for the  meal.  How is that for organization?!

I thought this pumpkin cheesecake looked heavenly (and yes, the recipe for it is in the book.)

There is also a Southern style Thanksgiving featured….that includes these divine cheddar chive biscuits.

And this Thanksgiving book also includes a plan for a day after turkey-day (aka Black Friday) lunch.  

There is also a weekend breakfast meal with the menu, recipes, centerpiece idea, and work plan – all very do-able.  The last chapter is about elements of the Thanksgiving table from dinnerware, to glasses, to serving pieces.

I just loved this book when I bought it several years ago, but I could not find the accompanying Christmas one.  I searched and searched everywhere I could, but none could be found.  Then amazingly, one of my students (whose mother knew I was looking for it) found it and gave me the book for a Christmas gift!

Christmas Entertaining (Williams-Sonoma)

The first part of the book is a little more sophisticated than I like with a cocktail party, an Italian Christmas Eve, and a plan for Christmas in the City.  But after that it becomes much more home-style.  I loved the New England Christmas dinner with its beautiful table setting.

There is also a Snow Country Breakfast that is perfect for us (even if we never see that blanket of white here. :()

And Williams-Sonoma doesn’t stop with Christmas in this book.  There are also plans, menus, recipes, and all for a New Year’s Day Open House.

That meal includes the recipe for this gorgeous gingerbread with whipped cream.

So there you have it…two of my favorite books for the holidays.  The worn out copies in the photo at the top of the post are mine, but I will be giving one of you new copies. 🙂  Just leave a comment on this post and/or any of the ones that publish between now and midnight on Sunday, November 17, 2013.  You’ll be entered in the drawing (one comment per post please.)

I also wanted to tell you about another contest…one that Williams-Sonoma is hosting with Blackberry Farm.  (I did several posts on Blackberry Farm in the fall of 2012 that you can see here.)  You know I love that place (so I really shouldn’t be telling you about this contest since it decreases my chance of winning if you enter, right?) But this is too good not to mention.  They are giving away a two night stay for two including their fabulous dining, a culinary demonstration, a tour of the farm, and $1500 in cash.  You can enter it by clicking on the link below (scroll down to November 2.)

facebook.com/WilliamsSonoma

That brings us to the end of today’s post.  I will be back this week with several other book recommendations for you for holiday entertaining ideas.  Because these posts are part of a giveaway, I won’t be jumping in the comments this week (but I will be reading all of them.)  And I will leave you with a question to talk about today so that you can enter the book giveaway.

Where does your family celebrate Thanksgiving?facebook.com/blackberryfarm

November 9, 2013

Weekend Wandering in Cashiers, North Carolina

Happy Saturday!  Wouldn’t you love to be sitting in one of those rockers enjoying the view of the lake from there?  Me too…sigh.  That lake is Lake Glenville in Cashiers, a little town in the mountains of western North Carolina .  We have house and yard work to do this weekend, so perhaps I can take a virtual escape here for a few minutes.  Would you like to come along?  (Unless, of course, you’re lucky enough to already be there. 🙂 )

 chattoogaclub.com

I did a post on Chattooga Club located in Cashiers last spring, but I saved a few photos for now because they were just great eye candy for fall.  Chattooga is private.  That’s the entrance to their lodge above, and here’s a peek at their gorgeous dining room, also in the lodge.

chattoogaclub.com

Perfect for this season.  I would want to be seated at the table next to the cozy fireplace.  Wouldn’t you?

 chattoogaclub.com

Since Chattooga Club is private, and you will need a place to stay while you are there in Cashiers, perhaps you would like to rent the lake house with the porch pictured at the beginning of the post.  It is available through the innkeepers that run Elizabeth Pointe Lodge. (There is a post or two on it here.)  They have a large lake house and a smaller cottage, too.  (Information available here.)

nclakehouse.com

While in Cashiers, there are several great shops to visit.  I shared Francie Hargrove’s shop with you last month.  My all time favorite shop there is Rusticks (and if they would let me, I believe I could live in it. 🙂 ) It is also located on a lake.  Can’t get better than that!

rusticks.com

It was opened in 1992 by Ann and Rody Sherrill.  I remember their first little shop in a small upstairs area in Chestnut Square.  I thought I had died and gone to Heaven when I walked in it!  This shop at Canoe Point is huge and full of wonderful things.  It has such a great blend of rustic and refined (and the whitewashed walls are a beautiful backdrop for everything.)

Views Magazine

Views Magazine

There is also a shop in Cashiers that was opened recently by architect/designer Keven Hawkins.

gardenandgun.com

There have been a lot of rave reviews for this place!  I am looking forward to a trip when I can check it out.

lissyparker.com

**Edited on 11/11/13 – just heard from a reader that the Keven Hawkins shop may have closed.  So soon?  How sad.

And finally, there are a number of good restaurants in Cashiers, but I want you to check out this one:  Cornucopia.  That building has been a general store, a school, and several other things.

source

They offer soups, salads, sandwiches, burgers, and a few blue plate specials – casual and delicious.  (Just what I crave after shopping.)  A Sunday brunch and dinner a few nights a week are available, but they ARE a seasonal restaurant and will be closing for the season some time around Thanksgiving.  Besides the yummy food,  there is also a meat and cheese shop, and their wonderful open air dining area.  Care to enjoy your meal out there?  Love that bark siding!

cashierssojourner.com

And now I have to go grab my very late lunch (for real) and get back to house cleaning here.  I hope you have enjoyed wandering around Cashiers today.  It is really a charming little town.  I have a giveaway starting for you on Monday, so I hope to see you back here then!

Enjoy your weekend.

(even if you are working on your house, too! 🙂
