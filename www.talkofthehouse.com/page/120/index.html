Does Your Home Wear Pink?

source

It’s February…the month of love, and romance, and valentines. What better time to see a little eye candy with some pink, right?

It’s everywhere.

source

Unfortunately, I have a slight problem with showing you all of this….

1st-option.com

I don’t like pink. üôÅ

I really never have. ¬†I don’t remember wearing it at all as a child. ¬†There is not one dab of it inside our house, and if you read the post on our master bedroom closet, you saw there is no pink in all of my wardrobe.

bhg.com

Even after having two sons, I never bought our daughter pink clothing. ¬†I think she might have had one pink outfit as a toddler. ¬†And her nursery decor? ¬†Yellow walls, white furniture, and all the linens were white with a red cherry print. üôÇ

cottage-company.com

So this post is definitely for you. (My mother has probably fallen out of her chair at the sight of all this pink on the blog today, since she knows how I feel about the color.)

bhg.com

But that doesn’t mean that I don’t think these rooms are pretty. ¬†On the contrary, I do think they are very pretty. ¬†They look very feminine, but my tastes run towards more tailored spaces for our family. I like rooms that I feel both men and women would be comfortable in. But if you are a fan of pink and pretty, you might want to check out some of the rooms by Tracey Rapisardi, like the one below. ¬†She is one of the queens of pretty! üôÇ

traceyrapisardi.com

If I were to do a room in pink, it would have to be a little girl’s room like one of these.

homebunch.com

You may remember this pink stripe room from this year’s HGTV Dream Home.

(Have you entered to win it?)

hgtv.com

If we had on old Victorian home, pink would be quite fitting for the exterior.

source

turningpoint2.tumblr.com

Or…if we had this cute little cottage in Seaside, I wouldn’t mind it being the color of cotton candy. ¬†(By the way, the name of this cottage is Precious. üôÇ )

flickr.com

And Precious happens to be the guest cottage that goes with this pink home…Savannah Rose.

seaside.library.nd.edu

Now there is one color combination with pink, that I would consider using for a girl’s room in this house. ¬†And I probably like it because it seems more tailored….hot pink with navy.

potterybarnkids.com

I like that combination with a lot of white for contrast.

pbteen.com

You might remember that our daughter’s dorm room was done in hot pink, navy, and white last fall. ¬†(She still doesn’t wear much pink, but there is certainly pink in her decorating! üôÇ )

Last December, Talbots’ catalog featured a page with all of this clothing. ¬†I loved the combinations…tailored and feminine…and seriously thought about getting the vest and gloves (and perhaps the sweater and skirt. üôÇ )

talbots.com

Maybe there is hope for some pink in my life yet!

So…do you and your home wear pink?

We’d love to know. üôÇ

Talk of the Weekend #16

Do you remember in last week’s Talk of the Weekend, I mentioned that our weekend had a dinner party, a taking-down-of-all-things-Christmas from the photoshoot, and something more…and that it was¬†kind of busy? Well, this weekend I want to share with you the something more,¬†and I really should rename this post….

Welcome to Disney. üôÇ

You see, our daughter is quite lucky to be interning at Disney World for this college semester, and we moved her to her new Disney apartment last Sunday and Monday. ¬†She and her 5 new roommates will be¬†castmembers¬†(Disney has their own special word for everything) in the parks and resorts. (Our daughter will be in concierge at one of the resorts.)

We did not have time on this trip to enjoy the parks, but after we checked into our hotel on Sunday we headed over to Downtown Disney for the evening (and I left my camera at the hotel…darn it! ¬†Sorry for the fuzzy phone photos. üôÅ ) ¬† ¬†The flowers were amazing!! ¬†The geraniums were gorgeous! ¬†(My brain was so confused…We took down Christmas on Saturday, and I was looking at geraniums in full bloom on Sunday! ¬†And it was January??)

It is such a fun place, you can’t help but get sucked into all that Disney environment…

Huey, Dewey, and Louie (above)…

Minnie, Mickey, and Pluto (below)…

¬†and Stitch!

We had dinner at Fulton’s Crab House that evening…designed as an old riverboat. üôÇ

Here is a better photo from their instagram.

All of our meals were absolutely delicious; our waiter from London was great. ¬†I had steak skewers that had some very good pickled red onions with them. ¬†Our son said his grouper with crabmeat was one of the best dishes he has ever had, and my husband’s fried shrimp looked wonderful. ¬†Our daughter had yummy shrimp scampi. ¬†We wandered around Downtown Disney afterwards and then returned to the hotel.

Monday morning, our daughter had to check in with Disney and do paperwork, so the rest of us went out for breakfast at one of the best breakfast places in Orlando…Keke’s. ¬†Here is the banana, nut, and caramel French toast I devoured. üôÇ

Afterwards we had a little time to kill, so of course we went in the nearby¬†Mall at Millenia, and of course I had to check out a few of my favorite stores. üôÇ

pottery barn kids…

Pottery Barn…

and Williams Sonoma.

I was in Crate and Barrel when our daughter called with the message that they were all ready to move everything in the apartment. ¬†So off we went.

¬†We headed back home but stopped at the Tommy Bahama Restaurant for a late afternoon meal on our way out of Orlando.
