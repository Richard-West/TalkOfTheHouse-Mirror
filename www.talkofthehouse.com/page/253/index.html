Christmas Traditions – Photographs and… Chalkboards?

Goodness what a busy string of days this has been!  We made it through the faculty party here, and the last day of school before our break was today.  Do you know how excited a room full of 8 year olds can be 6 days before Christmas????  Soooooo glad for Christmas vacation.  I hope I am not too late to show you a little more of our Christmas decorating.  I wanted to show you the playroom, and would you believe it?  There is not one speck of red in this room! (What’s gotten into me??)

There is a baby sized tree in there.  It is standing in a metal beverage bucket from Target and is decorated with clipped on black and white photographs of the children  – mostly scenes with Santa or snow.  I added tags I made out of scraps of black cardstock and hole reinforcers and used ribbon to hang them.

There is also black ribbon and white rick rack hanging all around it as our tree garland (and gingham ribbon at the top.)   The star is simply 2 pieces of cardstock cut out in a star shape and glued together with an opening to slide over the top of the tree.  I used chalk for the writing on it and on the tags.

The wrapped gifts have black cardstock tags to look like chalkboards.  I’ve got to get some more rickrack to add to them tomorrow.(They look a little plain-Jane to me.)

There is more of the “photographs and chalkboard combination” as you come in the door to the playroom.

A wreath is hanging over the chalkboard that normally lives there, and I added one more photograph of the boys with Santa and a gingham bow.

And there are cookies for Santa with a little chalkboard-like tag.

I just checked the weather forecast to see if a White Christmas might be coming our way, but no such luck…just rain for Christmas Eve. Santa better have an umbrella!  I still have a good bit of shopping and wrapping to do in the next few days, but at least now there is some time to do it.  How is your shopping, wrapping, and decorating coming along?  Getting close to being done?

with Christmas cheer,

December 13, 2012

Christmas Traditions – Greenery Outside and the Giveaway Winner!

I am thinking all of you want to know who won the giveaway more than you want to see greenery, so let’s take care of that first, shall we?

Your comments on the last 6 posts have just been amazing!  I really should print them all out and save them in a little book.  I have loved all your stories of reading and baking and decorating and your memories of your childhood Christmas stockings…all wonderful tales.  If you have not read the comments, I am telling you that you should.  (That’s the bossy teacher in me coming out. :)) I say all this humbly to tell you “thank you.”  Thank you for reading my posts, entering my little giveaways, and sharing your memories and sweet comments with me.  I am touched.

And now to the winner….

In an effort to be quick and efficient, I just totaled up all the comments from the previous Christmas Traditions posts. There were 231 comments in all.  I used a random number generator…and the lucky number was 15.

That comment belonged to ……..me.  Can you believe it????  It was my comment to everyone on the snow post.  Too funny!  So I did it again.  (What?  You thought I would keep it all for myself?  No way.) Here was my second attempt at using the generator.

34 happens to be the number that my husband wore on all his football jerseys.  Must be a lucky number!

This was super sweet comment number 34:

So Pat, I will be getting in touch with you about your prize.  I have another giveaway in mind for January, so everyone else, please stick around!

And now to today’s post.  I thought we would go outside and take a look at the Christmas garlands and wreaths hanging on our home’s exterior.  Our warm spell has left briefly, and it was a little cold trying to take these photographs. (I am not complaining…perhaps we can finally have a fire in the fireplace.)

Years ago, I made my garlands from greenery I cut in the woods.  It killed my hands! (No, I didn’t wear gloves like I should have.)  It was a major job, but I did it because I could not find any faux greenery that I liked at the time.

Since then, I have found one that is an acceptable replacement from Hobby Lobby.  It looks somewhat like real fir, and it certainly saves me a lot of time to use premade garlands.  The only problem is storing all of them…they take up a great deal of space.

I do use fresh cut wreaths on the doors and inside the house.  Here is a little better look at the door.

And here is the other door.

I don’t think I have shown you our still unfinished-on-the-inside garage.  (It’s getting there slowly.)  I put up garlands and bows on it too, but my husband does all the upper ones since I have a terrible fear of heights.

It has been somewhat cloudy here at the best time of day to photograph the house.  So here is one from last year that looks better than any I’ve taken lately.  It has the same garlands, but I did replace all the bows this year. (You can’t tell that in the photos though.)

And finally, here is one at dusk.  You can see the little Christmas tree in the upper window.  There is only attic space up there, but we keep the tree in the window all year.  The switch to turn on its lights is in the laundry room of all places!

When we first moved in, our trees in the front yard were small enough that I could cover them with little white Christmas lights (using a ladder of course.)  Now that they have grown so much it is impossible, but I do miss having some twinkling lights on the trees outside.  So my question to you today is actually two-fold.

 Do you put up lights on the exterior of your home, and do you use white or multi-color lights?

I hope you have a fun filled December weekend.  I have a to-do list a mile long since we have our faculty Christmas party here next week. (And my tree is still standing naked in the great-room!)

with Christmas cheer,
