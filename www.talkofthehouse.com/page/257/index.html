The Inn at Half Mile Farm

source

Happy Thanksgiving!  I told you the other day I would have a less thought provoking post for you today and that I would share my favorite Highlands inn with you.  So just sick back, relax, and enjoy my favorite … The Inn at Half Mile Farm.  I wish I could just take you all there!

bnbfinder.com

It sits a few miles outside of town – giving it a more secluded, peaceful atmosphere.  We have not been to Highlands in a few years, and it was owned by Jack Henry and his wife when we last visited.  They were the brainchild behind the project – buying the farm, renovating it, and opening it as an inn in 2001.  We visited it several times in its early years before it was sold to the Messer family.  My in-laws ended up building a cabin near there, so we stayed at their cabin more often than in Highlands itself for awhile.

halfmilefarm.com

When I first read the inn had been sold, I was a little worried.   But all the reviews since then have been superb, so the Messers must be doing a wonderful job with it.  (By the way, that porch above has great views of the pond that sits down the hill from it.)

To get to Half Mile Farm, you drive a few miles out of Highlands, turn right on a road, and then follow a dirt road back through the woods to the inn…nicely isolated.  We parked the car and entered the farmhouse from the back.  You walk through a plank walled hallway to the innkeeper’s desk, and right off the hall is this comfortably pretty common area.  They also serve wine and hors d’oeuvres there in the evening.

halfmilefarm.com

On the other side of the hall is the breakfast room.  A heavy breakfast is served each morning for their guests, and I loved their baked eggs with tarragon and leeks so much that Jack gave me the recipe.  Of course their recipe calls for 30 eggs, and it feeds 13!  They do have a cookbook, so perhaps I need to order that to see if they have the recipe scaled down to a family sized portion.

halfmilefarm.com

The entire complex is made up of the restored farmhouse, 2 new additional buildings housed around the courtyard, and a number of very old log cabins that were moved onto the 14 acre property and restored.  There are 4 rooms available in the farmhouse.  Here is one:

halfmilefarm.com

This is the courtyard behind the original farmhouse where you can see some of the new additions.  There is also a swimming pool not far from here.

idoeventsatlanta.com

My favorite rooms are in the new additions.  How do you like this one with its painted bed and floral fabrics?

halfmilefarm.com

Here is one of the new bathrooms.  (All of the bathrooms in the farmhouse have also been renovated and beautifully modernized.)

mywedding.com

This is the room that we have stayed in several times.  (Naturally, I would want the room with the red checked fabric.)

hotels.com

orbitz.com

But the next time we go, I hope we will stay in this room.  (By the way, all of the rooms in the farmhouse and in the additions are named after waterfalls in the area.)

halfmilefarm.com

When we were there, I did not see any of the cabins that have been moved in and restored.  From what I have read online, they look like they would be fantastic places to stay.

halfmilefarm.com

halfmilefarm.com

halfmilefarm.com

And you should know by now, if I have a snow photo I have to include it!halfmilefarm.com

Not only is my post today my Thanksgiving one, but it also marks my 100th post since starting this blog.  I wanted to let you know that on this Thanksgiving, I am thankful for many things…one if which is you.  I so enjoy doing this, and I am thankful that you all are out there spending your time here, reading my words, and talking with me about houses and anything else I write about.  I hope your Thanksgiving is full of food, family, and fun, and that you have renewed energy for whatever you are doing on Friday and the coming weekend…like maybe……

…….decorating for Christmas?halfmilefarm.com

with a grateful heart for all of you,

Highlands’ Old Edwards Inn

source

With the exception of Acorns, I only showed you one side of the main street in Highlands, NC on my Weekend Wandering post the other day.  There was a reason for that, and today I want to tell you the story of the other side.  And I am not real sure how I feel about this story.  See what you think.

google.com

For many years, my sister and her husband would make an annual trip to Highlands and stay at the then charming Old Edwards Inn.  It was old. It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places, and it probably needed renovating at the time.  But it was right in the middle of downtown, and it had a nice restaurant…and a great outdoor dining area at which I frequently enjoyed a bowl of some of the best she crab soup I have ever tasted.  (This was long before I developed my allergy to shell fish.)

Anyway, someone else, or more likely, a group of someones, decided that they loved the Old Edwards Inn, and they also loved Highlands.  So they bought the inn and began investing millions of dollars in it.  It turned out beautiful.  They left the side with the old restaurant looking much as it did before the renovation – but better.

oldedwardsinn.com

The outdoor dining area is now the “Wine Garden.”cntraveler.com

This is the new and improved lobby area.orbitz.com

This is one of several dining areas they now have.oldedwardsinn.com

If you happen to be there this week for Thanksgiving and would like to dine with them, here is their special Thanksgiving menu.  Their restaurant has won many awards.

oldedwardsinn.com

I know what you really want to see are the rooms, right?  Here are a few, and they are lovely.

 

oldedwardsinn.com

oldedwardsinn.com

oldedwardsinn.com

They have not only renovated the entire inn, but they have also built a spa there.  I believe the last room above is one of the spa suites.  It has truly become a world class place to stay – with numerous awards given by the leading travel magazines.

Now here is where I don’t quite know where I stand on the story.  What they did with the Old Edwards Inn is a work of art.  It’s gorgeous.  But then…they began buying up other places in Highlands – and giving them their architectural “stamp.”  They bought and renovated the old Kelsey and Hutchinson place a block behind them and turned it into The Lodge at the Old Edwards Inn.  (We stayed there when it was K&H, and yes, it did need renovating.)

clarkzookarchitects.com

clarkzookarchitects.com

Here is a peek at the interior of the lodge.oldedwardsinn.com

And yes it looks a zillion times better, too.

The group also bought up parcels of land downtown and built cottages that you can stay in.  They have the look of the Old Edwards Inn.

oldedwardsinn.com

oldedwardsinn.com

My favorite project that they have done is a mile or two outside of Highlands.  They have a house they call The Farm.  It can be rented for special occasions – mostly weddings.

rusticweddingchic.com

It has beautiful gardens on the grounds there.oldedwardsinn.com

The interior spaces of the Farm are very charming.  I love this kitchen.oldedwardsinn.com

oldedwardsinn.com

oldedwardsinn.com

If you would like to see some gorgeous photos of a wedding there, click here.  It shows another of the bedrooms at the Farm.

There is also a “barn” at The Farm.  This is how it looked a year or so ago.

oldedwardsinn.com

rusticweddingchic.com

And this is how the interior used to look inside, (and it may still…I’m just not sure….)

oldedwardsinn.com

I’m not sure because these were the plans for the renovation of The Barn.iloveswmag.com

And this is what a reception in The Barn looks like.oldedwardsinn.com

So everything they have done is very beautiful and first class, but there are a few things about it that bother me.  First, I guess if all of these projects had been done by several different groups of investors, I wouldn’t feel as if it were a monopoly gobbling up the little town parcel by parcel.  And they are taking it parcel by parcel.  One of the oldest Christmas shops used to be housed in an old white building.  Here it is today after the Old Edwards Inn group left their mark on it.

weddingmapper.com

Yes, it looks like a nicer building, but it doesn’t look like the Highlands that I remember.  But should it?  What is progress?  And how is this any different than architectural covenants or resorts in other towns?  See that is why I can’t quite decide if this is good or not.

The other thing about it is it seems to be slowly changing into a town only for the rich. (I think it did start out that way many years ago.) If money were no object, I would choose to stay in one of their cottages, but for most of us, money is an object.  As each little parcel is put in their stash, the prices go up. I am afraid that eventually Highlands will no longer be a quaint little town, but instead, a resort for the wealthy.  That does bother me.

The other day, I showed you the other side of the street first so that you could get a feel for the old Highlands.  There is still this inn on that side, across the street from the Old Edwards Inn.  I hope they are able to keep their quaint charm.

destinationnexus.com

And there is this wonderful church on the same side of the street as the Old Edwards Inn.highlandsinfo.com

Surely they won’t gobble it up.

I have one more post for you on Highlands that I will share later this week…not quite as thought provoking as this one though. 🙂  It will be about our favorite place to stay there – and it is charming.

Meanwhile, I’d love to hear your thoughts on the pros and cons of development in an old town.

until next time,
