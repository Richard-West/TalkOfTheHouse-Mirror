May 24, 2013

A Gray Shingled Cottage in Seaside

source

Happy weekend!  I hope some of you are lucky enough to be spending this Memorial Day weekend at the beach.  We are going to be lucky enough to spend it…painting the room over the garage. 🙂 (I am happy to be making progress on the space…vacation will come later.)  Today I want to share with you how I discovered Idyll-by-the-Sea, the beautiful beach house in my last post.

Several years ago, I was wandering the streets of my favorite little beach town – Seaside, Florida.  It was full of cute cute cottages all painted various shades of pastel colors.


taxi-destin.com

All had names painted on signs hung on white picket fences…

source

I was snapping pictures right and left, admiring the architecture of all the houses, when I came upon this one that stopped me in my tracks….

source

The Nantucket style of a gray shingled house has always been my very favorite, and to find this one in that sea of pastel cottages was a wonderful surprise.  I know you can’t read the name on the fence, but it was Idyll by the Sea.  This was the original Idyll by the Sea.  The one in my last post is actually Idyll by the Sea Two.  Jerry Jongerious and Carol Irvine planned this home for seven years and were on a waiting list for this special lot in Seaside for a long time, too.  Richard Gibbs was the architect who helped them bring this dream of a home to reality.  Just look at some of the architectural details it wore….

seaside.library.nd.edu

seaside.library.nd.edu

I found their website for this rental house years ago and was thrilled to enjoy all the virtual tours they had for the rooms inside.   The main entrance was on the lower level where the bedrooms were located.  White walls of bead board and ALL those windows were what I loved most about Idyll by the Sea.  If you read the previous post and if you click on the link below and look at the tour for this area, you may spot some of the antique furnishings they now use in their beach house in North Carolina.

idyll-by-the-sea.com

Since we are in this downstairs area, let me just show you a couple of  the four bedrooms.  You will see groupings of plates on the walls of the bedrooms here just as they have in their other home, and you may remember this beautiful antique bed.

idyll-by-the-sea.com

Here is another gorgeous bed that was in the master bedroom.

idyll-by-the-sea.com

There was a fireplace with a sitting area in the master bedroom.  I seem to remember seeing that same chair and ottoman in another bedroom in their North Carolina house.

idyll-by-the-sea.com

And here was the master bathroom.

idyll-by-the-sea.com

Also on the lower level you would have found this porch right off the master bathroom.  Goodness that looks like a relaxing place to be!

idyll-by-the-sea.com

If you were to go back inside and up the stairs you would have found one more small bedroom.

idyll-by-the-sea.com

Just like Idyll by the Sea Two, this home had a true great room with the living, dining, and kitchen areas all open to each other. This occupied the rest of the upper floor.

idyll-by-the-sea.com

You would have seen the same antique herb drying table in the dining area that they now have for dining in the North Carolina house.  (The one that seats 14.)

idyll-by-the-sea.com

If you had walked out the doors of the great room, you would have found yourself here….

idyll-by-the-sea.com

with this view.idyll-by-the-sea.com

And just look at the detail in the railing around that porch and deck area!  The cutouts on it followed a theme of sun, stars, and dolphins.

idyll-by-the-sea.com

Besides the wonderful house itself, Idyll by the Sea also had a charming little yard…complete with an herb garden for you to use during your stay there.

idyll-by-the-sea.com

Unfortunately, the owners sold the original Idyll by the Sea and moved all the furnishings and unique accessories to their North Carolina beach house. I really loved this one in Seaside though.  I think the smaller size was more cozy than their big beach house, but if they have a large family, I can see how the North Carolina home better fits their needs.  (Plus they are still using all their beautiful furnishings.)

The new owners changed the name to Forever More, brought in their new furnishings, and now offer it as a rental.  You can find information about it here.

sandpipervacationrentals.com

So now you know the story of both Idyll by the Sea cottages.  I hope your weekend is full of the start of summertime fun, and perhaps you are lucky enough to be staying in a charming beach front cottage.  (If so, I am jealous!)  Enjoy your weekend…wherever you may be. 🙂

idyll-by-the-sea.com

until next time…

May 22, 2013

A Beautiful and Unique Beach House off the Coast of North Carolina

source

Oh yes…another beach house today.  When the weather starts warming up, and summer vacation is this close, I just can’t resist.  But this one today is not a cute little beach cottage.  This is a beautiful BIG one. They seem to build them majorly big on some of the islands off the North Carolina coast.  And unlike the Nights in Rodanthe house in my last post, this one has not been used as a movie set…but I think it would make a great one.  This one is in North Topsail Beach.  I am not going to tell you the story of how I know about it yet (no, I have not stayed there).  I am saving that story for my next post. 🙂

This home is named Idyll-by-the-Sea by its owners, Jerry Jongerius and Carol Irvine.  Once you see the rooms inside, I am sure you will agree with me that they must have a true love of antiques and a wonderful knack for finding unusual treasures for their home.  They also either have a lot of computer knowledge in website design, or they just have a great web designer they have hired because they have had the most wonderful virtual tours on their site for many many years.  So I am linking most of today’s photos to the tours of the rooms.  I hope you will click on them and take a look around. (They may take a little longer to load.) Let’s start in the foyer by the main entrance.

idyll-by-the-sea.com

Most of the beach homes that I love have white walls, but you will often find stained or natural wood plank walls in the beach homes off the North Carolina coast. So you will see that the owners have used them throughout their home.  This one has over 6,000 square feet (I told you it was big!), and it has four wood burning fireplaces.  Next up is the great room.  You could really seat a crowd in there!  And isn’t that antique wet bar beautiful?!  This home is really meant for entertaining a crowd.

idyll-by-the-sea.com

You can see that the dining area is right on the other side of the great room.  It too can handle a crowd with its big table – 14 guests to be exact.  Yes, that is quite an unusual piece of art there in the corner!

idyll-by-the-sea.com

Here is another view of that area and on into the fully equipped kitchen.

idyll-by-the-sea.com

idyll-by-the-sea.com

Off of this large great room, dining, and kitchen area is this fantastic ocean viewing porch.  Who needs any other room when you have this wonderful space??

idyll-by-the-sea.com

Now let’s talk about the bedrooms.  There are four levels in Idyll by the Sea with 7 bathrooms and 8 bedrooms.  It can sleep 18 people!  I think of it like a mini-inn there on the beach. 🙂  The entire 4th floor is the master spa suite.  There is a steam shower, a dry cedar sauna, and a jetted tub.

idyll-by-the-sea.com

There is also a second master bedroom (in case you have other couples with you on your vacation.)

idyll-by-the-sea.com

 I’m not going to show all the bedrooms here today, but here are a few more for you to enjoy…

Bedroom #1 with its ocean view…

idyll-by-the-sea.com

A bedroom for little boys…(I love the dog art up in this room!)

idyll-by-the-sea.com

And then there is this precious pink one for little girls…

idyll-by-the-sea.com

A porch is on every level, and the bedroom floor is no exception. Take a look at the porch you find right out the bedroom doors.  (I don’t think there is any detail the Irvines have overlooked.)

idyll-by-the-sea.com

Besides all the porches, there are many more outside spaces for guests to enjoy…a hot tub, an outdoor kitchen, an outdoor fireplace, and a private pool.  (See why I said it was like a mini-inn?)  And here is the walkover that takes you straight to the beach.

idyll-by-the-sea.com

Idyll by the Sea is one of seven rental homes Jerry and Carol own.  They are spread out from Washington state to Kissimmee, Florida (outside Disney World.)  Each has a unique style of decor. While I do love this one, it is out of my budget for my family for a week, but if we had several families staying there together (remember it sleeps 18) then it would probably be affordable.  I can see it being perfect for a family reunion.  Can’t you?

idyll-by-the-sea.com

When we go on vacation for a week, we often stay in rental homes (much smaller ones.)  Which brings me to today’s question for you dear readers…

When you go on vacation, what type of accommodation do you most frequently stay in?

(hotel, motel, B&B, rental home, RV, etc.)

We’d love to know!
