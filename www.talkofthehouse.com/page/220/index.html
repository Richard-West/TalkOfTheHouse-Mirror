Weekend Wandering in Watersound

source

If you travel a few miles up the road from Seaside, you will come to another planned development named Watersound – not to be confused with Watercolor – which is a few miles the opposite direction.  Years ago, when I first read about the plans for Watersound, I was beyond thrilled.

southwaltonluxuryhomes.com

To say that I love the beaches in that area, would be an understatement.  No offense to any other coastal region, but those Gulf beaches in the Panhandle area of Florida with their sugar white sand are the most beautiful to me.  But as far as coastal architecture goes, my favorite has long been the style you find in New England.  My two loves came together in Watersound.

wcjarchitect.com

It is a community of buildings in that wonderful old weathered shingle and shake style.  We drove through the development  in its early years when only a few homes dotted its landscape.  I thought they were gorgeous, and many more have been built since then.

desin-florida-real-estate.com

Everywhere you look there are shingled homes with contrasting trim.  I couldn’t ask for a larger dose of my favorite architecture.

homeson30a.com

One of the first homes built at Watersound Beach was this one.

nestegg.typepad.com

You might recognize it as the 2004 Idea House for Southern Accents magazine.  The nautical rooms in it were all designed by Suzanne Kasler and her team of talented people.  I liked this bright kitchen when it was featured back then.

myhomeideas.com

And you probably recognize this children’s space with its rope beds.

myhomeideas.com

myhomeideas.com

If you have $4,000,000 waiting to be invested, the home is for sale here.  Of course there are boardwalks to the ocean and pathways throughout Watersound.  Biking is encouraged.

watersoundbeachflorida.com

I had hoped that Watersound was going to be a development with the feel of a coastal “village” with shops, parks, and community pools – like nearby Seaside.  And while it does have restaurants and a gorgeous pool area (a 7,000 square foot pool at that!)…

watersoundbeachflorida.com

It is a resort not a village.  When they built a golf course and condos it changed the vibe for me.  I’m not saying those things are bad, I just thought I wanted Seaside with New England architecture on the Gulf coast.

homeaway.com

(Is it just me, or do the condos at Watersound remind you of these Yacht Club accommodations at Disney???)

Disney Yacht Club @ tripadvisor.com

I have to admit the Watersound condos do have beautiful rooms.  I love the wall treatment and the framed photos in this one!

watersoundvacationrentals.com

And this condo was featured in Coastal Living magazine several years ago.

watersoundbeach.net

vrbo.com

I believe Watersound has the longest stretch of beach of any of the developments there on 30a (a mile, if I am correct.) By building the condos more people can enjoy that beautiful beach, and it is often less expensive to vacation in one of those than renting a full house.  Plus many people don’t need the space that a home offers (but some of the condos are very large, too.)

Although these are very well done and not the old shag-carpet-and-sliding-glass-door condos I remember from years ago, I still prefer the charm of a house at the beach.  We almost rented this one at Watersound (below) many years ago but decided to  stay in a waterfront one at Seaside instead.  As gorgeous as this one was…and I truly mean gorgeous…I just couldn’t pass up oceanfront in Seaside.  Take a look at this one in Watersound…

vrbo.com

vrbo.com

vrbo.com

vrbo.com

vrbo.com

You can take a fun virtual tour of the house by clicking here.  The view of the water from there was really wonderful, and I should have made the decision to stay there.  It was brand new as a rental, and the owner was offering an unbelievable rate.  The Seaside cottage was wonderful too, but I don’t think we will ever find anything as good in Watersound ever again.

visitsouthwalton.com

There are many beautiful homes in Watersound.  In my next post, I want to share with you my favorite house there (designed by one of my favorite architectural firms! 🙂 ) And on this summer weekend, since you already know my favorite beach, I will leave this question for you to talk about….

What is your favorite beach?

I am looking forward to hearing from you!

A Designer to Love: Stephen Saint-Onge (part 3) The Interview

source

The interview??? Yes, dear readers, I have been holding out on you, but I really wanted to surprise you with this.  (And let me just say, it has been murder not to tell you!)  You remember last week when I did the first post about Stephen Saint-Onge, he surprised us all when he left his kind words in the comments and offered the autographed books.

designerdadstudio.com

Well… I also received an email giving me the opportunity to a  Q&A time with the Designer Dad.  At first I thought…man… I should ask for his advice on what to do with my challenging boys’ room (yes, it is still not done),  but then I decided to ask questions that all of you might want answered.  So go get a cup of coffee.  Then sit back, relax, and enjoy our time with Stephen Saint-Onge. 🙂

designerdadstudio.com

Kelly:  Hi Stephen!  Thank you so much for doing this for my readers here.  We know you have been interested in homes since an early age.  At what point in your life did you realize that you could have a career in home design?

Stephen Saint-Onge:  I used to watch old movies when I was a kid and draw the floor plans of the homes I was seeing in those classic films.  I suppose that was when something sparked – in some little way – that design would be or could be a part of my life.  It’s funny – because I still start out a new project pretty much the same way I did when I was a kid.  I’ll watch a favorite film and get inspired about a project.  Not so much about a theme or location – but about capturing a mood or the essence of what I want a project to become.

Kelly:  What is the most challenging part of your work?

Stephen Saint-Onge:   When I walk into any house – I see the potential of what can be done right away.  I see the end result.  Most homeowners cannot see the potential in their homes because they live in it everyday.  That is why I love doing in-home or Skype design consults with real homeowners.  The challenge is then backtracking from that end result vision and looking at each step that needs to be accomplished to take me from start to finish.  I suppose that vision of what I am aiming for keeps me on the pulse of what I’m looking to create.  I am a list man – so I keep moving quickly towards that goal to get it done.  In essence, getting it out of my head and bringing it to life.

designerdadstudio.com

Kelly:  You have said that your home is very “New England-ish” in style, could you describe it more?

Stephen Saint-Onge:  It’s a classic 1940’s farmhouse style home.  It reminds a bit of the home in “Mr. Blandings Builds His Dream Home,” but it also is very much like the homes I grew up around in Vermont.

Kelly:  What predominant colors have you used in your own home – both on the exterior and in the interiors?

Stephen Saint-Onge:  I love a classic white exterior on an older home.  I love dark green accents for exterior doors and shutters, but I have to say that I have recently started liking the contrast of black accents too for doors and shutters.  I just completed a private client project where I designed all the interior doors for the entire house and we painted them black.  Crisp and clean looking.  Inside, my favorite new color is “Jute” from Benjamin Moore.  I use their “Simply White” a great deal for trim and ceilings.  Tend to keep it pretty neutral inside.

designerdadstudio.com

Kelly:  What is your favorite space in your own personal home and why?

Stephen Saint-Onge:  In summer, I like my kitchen because I enjoy cooking with my family and friends.  I like the casual feel of the room, and it is inviting because everyone ends up in that room.  I also like it because there is always music playing in there.  I suppose it is the room that affects all my senses…great natural light, smell of coffee brewing in the morning, roasting turkey at Thanksgiving, various levels of lighting for entertaining, vegetable garden outside the window, the dog is always there and there is always activity with the kids around.

designerdadstudio.com

Kelly:  We know you are a New Englander, is there another place that you would like to build a home for you and your family?

Stephen Saint-Onge:  I travel a great deal for work, so I have seen many places around the country.  I like the natural beauty of what New England has to offer, but I also enjoy the vibe in and around New York City too where I work a great deal of the time.  I suppose from a design perspective I could foresee a style of home that would tap into the various places I have been drawn to over the years – Miami for a tropical setting, Malibu for that beach house style or Montana for that big open sky cabin setting.

designerdadstudio.com

Kelly:  Every space is different in its style and lighting.  Do you have a go-to paint color that you have found works well in many settings?

Stephen Saint-Onge:  Not one particular color.   Like I mentioned before, I did that house with black doors throughout.  In that house, we did a great deal of white walls or pale neutrals throughout.  I liked that the setting was a classic farm house style, but the black and white gave it a modern feel – matched with reclaimed wood, industrial lighting and other accents.

Kelly:  It seems that each year, the home décor industry bombards us with new trends that leave as fast as they come.  How do you update a home so that it remains current but can withstand the test of time without falling prey to the latest trends? 

Stephen Saint-Onge:  Classic choices.  I compare it to fashion.  If you buy a classic suit – it stands the test of time.  If you go trendy – you tire of it easily, or fashion moves on to the next best thing.  It’s the same with home design.  You can make classic choices for the foundation pieces in a room like a sofa or dining table, but you can always add color or accessories around those main pieces to change up the mood or style a bit.  The key is making things easy to change up – like new throw pillows, simple artwork, an area rug, lamps – things that do not break the bank but allow the foundation pieces to remain.

designerdadstudio.com

Kelly:  You have a true love of art and photography.  Have you taken photography classes?  What kind of camera did you begin with?

Stephen Saint-Onge:  I love movies – so photography was a natural progression for me.  Through design I can create a setting or mood and with photography I can capture it.   I have worked with many photographers over the years on photo shoots for makeovers I have done or campaigns that I have been involved in – so I’ve learned from them along the way.  I love being behind the lens.  When the setting is right, and the natural lighting is working – I can click away.  I just shot a makeover of a dining room, and I took so many images of it because the sun was setting outside.  The mood of the room kept evolving, and I was inspired to bring it to life in the images.

Camera maker….Canons all the way.  The first images I ever shot were with an old Canon with film.  I still use a Canon today – though I have upgraded from that original one to a digital model.

designerdadstudio.com

Kelly:  You also seem to really love cooking.  What is your favorite dish/meal to prepare?

Stephen Saint-Onge:  To me, preparing a meal is a great de-stresser at the end of the day or something fun to do on the weekend.  You can have a hectic day and come home and cook a meal.  It’s something that is attainable and comforting.  There is a beginning, a middle and an end to preparing a meal.  I like that – you can get an end result.  It’s doable in a short time and you can appreciate what you accomplished in that time.

Also, I enjoy the presentation part of the meal.  You can create a big meal or even a simple meal, but it’s about setting the stage.  Music is key – various styles of music for whatever you are in the mood for.  Lighting is also important – levels of light make a meal inviting.  Cut some flowers from the garden and put them in a Mason jar, or gather some lemons in a bowl as a centerpiece…  Lay the food out on simple white plates or bowls.. Meals are a chance to be good to yourself and your family.  So if the meal is elaborate like Thanksgiving or a simple salad on a summer night – set the stage to make it good for you. The dining room makeover I just shot was all about “gathering place” – reminding us all to enjoy the simpler things in life over a meal:  home, family and friends.

Favorite meal.  An old standby is a roasted chicken with olive oil, Kosher salt and lemons.  Always a big hit.  Big fan of making up soups as well.  In winter, I’ll cook up a spin on my grandmother’s chicken soup or some spicy curry stew.  However, as basic as it is – my favorite meal could possibly be chocolate chip cookies and a glass of milk.

designerdadstudio.com

Kelly:  We know that you, like many of us, study television and movie “homes” for the details that we can emulate in our own spaces.  Can you name some specific examples where you have taken a movie detail and put it into use in a space?

Stephen Saint-Onge:  I designed a living room fireplace that had been brick and made it over with stone veneers to recreate the look of the living room in the film “Out of Africa.”  I designed a kitchen for a family with some inspiration from the kitchen that everyone loves in the film “Somethings Gotta Give.”  I am designing a private residence right now that has a foyer with bookcases in it much like the foyer set from the television show “The Good Wife.”

It’s funny on my site – I did a few behind-the-scenes features of the homes seen on the shows “The Good Wife,” “Downton Abbey,” “Parenthood” and “Blue Bloods” – it was amazing to hear how people were really into being inspired by the homes they saw on television or in a film.  My personal favorite film homes:  Out of Africa, The Holiday, Christmas In Connecticut, Holiday Inn and Under The Tuscan Sun.

myhomeideas.com

Kelly:  I love all of the movies you just named – especially Out of Africa.  And it has such a gorgeous soundtrack, too.  Your blog includes beautiful music.  Do you have a favorite genre that you listen to when working, or do you need quiet to do your best work?

Stephen Saint-Onge:  Always have music going.  I am a big fan of movie soundtracks – just because when I’m working in my studio – they inspire me, and that is usually what I tend to listen to.  Pandora Radio is something that I love on my iPad and MAC.  I can change things up easily as the mood strikes me with my various channels.  Mumford and Sons is a current favorite, Carla Bruni, Secret Garden, U2, Keith Urban, Mary Chapin Carpenter….It changes all the time.  For dinner prep it could be Mumford and Sons, then for dinner it could be French Cafe music or simple piano – then for clean up it’s Glen Hansard music or Van Morrison.

designerdadstudio.com

Kelly:  What job or assignment of yours has been your favorite and why?

Stephen Saint-Onge:  Designing a makeover of a community room for a Boys and Girls’ Club in Brooklyn, NY.  These inner-city teens used this room everyday that was so beat up and worn out.  It was depressing and filled with broken furniture.   I called out to some vendors, and I went in there for three days and made over the entire room with all new things.  In the end, it looked liked a high-end hotel lobby and we revealed it to the kids on live TV.  I loved that.  It affected their lives in a big way.  That is what I like doing with design and what I do.  I’m trying to put together a possible makeover for a community still affected by Hurricane Sandy – so I’m hoping we can create some magic for some other people soon.

Kelly:  Those sound like wonderful projects.  Is there a particular designer whose work you admire?

Stephen Saint Onge:  I’m a big fan of Thomas O’Brien.  I like his design style and way of putting rooms together.  Ralph Lauren is someone that gets how to create a complete lifestyle.  He empowers people with the fact that it is not just about how you look or dress but what you surround yourself with too.  I have learned a lot from how he brings things to life.

source

Kelly:  I know this is totally unrelated to design, but I have a love for old trucks.  Your old truck is wonderful. What make and model is it? 

Stephen Saint-Onge:  The blue truck is a vintage Dodge truck.  I have a real soft spot for vintage pick up trucks.  Classic and simple in design.  I wish the car companies would just make a limited edition pick up truck.  There is something very all-American about them… A reminder of the simpler times.

Kelly:  Speaking of simpler times, what is your favorite family vacation?

Stephen Saint-Onge:  Beach time along the ocean.

designerdadstudio.com

 Kelly:  What is the next big thing for you on your agenda of upcoming projects?

Stephen Saint-Onge:  Exploring some television projects.  Starting to think about a new book.

Kelly:  And my final question for you is this… What is on your bucket list?
