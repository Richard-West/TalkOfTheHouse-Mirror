Decorating Our Daughter’s College Apartment…and some diy art

Our daughter is a senior at her college and due to a critical housing shortage, her school has booted all the seniors off campus.  I am not happy about it because I think you get so much more out of living on campus, but she is more than happy to have an off campus apartment.

This past semester while our daughter was doing her internship at Disney, her roommate checked out many many apartments in the city.  She narrowed the choices down to a few, and when our daughter returned home in May we all went “apartment touring” one Saturday.

This is the one we all agreed upon.  It is a two bedroom two bath apartment.  I really like the flooring in the bathrooms.

I wanted you to see all the “befores” before I show you the “afters.”  I forgot to take a photo of the kitchen “before,” but here is the “after” (not much different anyway.)  The kitchen is super tiny.

Our original decorating plan had been a color scheme of hot pink, kelly green, and black with Kate Spade type accents.  We looked everywhere for things but kept coming up empty handed on finding that particular green. Then one day in June, my daughter and I found a placemat with a paisley print that we loved.  It became the springboard for a different look.  We totally changed direction and went with blues for fabric and furniture with some metallic gold thrown in for an accent.  Here is how their dining room looks for the “after.”  Oh, and the lamp is the one I purchased at the New Perry Hotel’s liquidation sale.  I gave it a new finish with antique gold Rub ‘n Buff.

You can kind of see the placemat in the photo of the table below.  My mother donated the dining room furniture for the apartment.  (She scored all of it in yardsales! 🙂 )

Target also had a shower curtain in the colors and print we wanted for the bathroom.

And that little gold table was the perfect accent with towels.

Royal blue and aqua towels add a nice touch of color with the white.

Now here is my favorite part (and the parts that took so darn long to finish!) The beds were originally bunkbeds from Cargo looooooong ago for our sons.  They had a rail design all the way around them.  At some point in their life (the beds’ – not the boys’ 🙂 ) my husband covered the head and footboards with bead board, and I painted them black.  We used them like that for a number of years, but after our sons went off to college the beds just went into storage.  I pulled them out and painted them aqua this summer.  Don’t you just love how they turned out?!

We kept looking and looking for a bright blue quilt or comforter, and we finally had good luck at Bed, Bath, and Beyond.  This quilt and sham set is actually made by Fiesta – yes, the dish company!  The aqua dotted throw pillows came from Target. And again we found a gold and glass table there – this time to go between the beds.

My favorite thing about the room is the book page art. I had seen a similar thing done on canvas at Sugarboo. (You can see it here.)  That one cost $400, but we weren’t about to invest that much in art for our daughter’s apartment.  My husband typed up the quote and had it printed out on 24″ by 36″ paper.  I believe you could probably do the same by taking a document to Staples or other office supply stores and have them enlarge it.

The frames are merely large poster frames from Walmart.  I used Rub ‘n Buff antique gold to transform the plastic into a new look.  The total cost of each was less than $20….much better than $400. 🙂

Now let me tell you about the desk.  This is the desk we bought at the New Perry Hotel’s liquidation sale.

 Remember it?

 We painted it white, and I used Rub ‘n Buff again on some new hardware from Lowe’s.  The upper hutch part was a black painted hutch leftover from one of our son’s pieces of furniture.  The bottom had collapsed, but we kept the top knowing we could use it again.  That lamp was an old brass one that was also given a coat of antique gold Rub ‘n Buff.  (Can you tell I love that stuff?)  My husband made the desk chair from the parts of 2 broken chairs, and my mother made the cushion from fabric from an extra drapery panel. (I purchased an extra one to sew strips to the bottom of the two panels that were a little too short for the window.)  Got all that? 🙂  Anyway, here is the new/old desk.

I thought it turned out pretty darn good. 🙂

The blue and aqua color scheme continues in the living area with the throw pillows.  (The bright blue ones came from TJ Maxx, and the aqua ones came from Target.)  The sofa was the Ikea one we had in our playroom.  (Our daughter says she is going to return it to us when she moves out of the apartment.) The loveseat belongs to her roommate, and the blue rug came from Target. (Where else? 🙂 )

We did more book page art in the living room…this time with our daughter’s roommates’s choice of quotes. Although these frames are a little different from the ones in the bedroom, they also came from Walmart (for around $8 each) and they got their share of the antique gold Rub ‘n Buff as well.

 I wish I had taken a photo of the coffee table before my husband painted it.  It was purchased by my mother in law at the Hotel sale.  It had a scratched up cherry finish.

And here is their balcony overlooking….the parking lot. 🙁  But at least it does have 2 nice rocking chairs!

And that concludes our tour.  Can you believe there is not one dab of red nor one speck of gingham in the entire apartment?!  A miracle!  I am just happy that it is finished. Now onto our law school son’s super tiny apartment in Connecticut..I don’t know if it will even be photo worthy enough to show you.  We’ll see. His request to me was, “Mom, I want it functional – not decorated.”   (Ha. I think it can be both! 😉 )

The New Perry Hotel and… a Giveaway for You!

If it is alright with you, I am going to go all sentimental today.  I want to tell you about a hotel – The New Perry Hotel in Perry, Georgia.  The hotel is anything but new. In fact it is quite old.  Its location was the site of another hotel – Cox’s Inn, built in the 1850’s as a stagecoach stop.  Its name was changed to The Perry Hotel in the 1880’s when a railroad was built between Perry and nearby Fort Valley, Georgia.

I’ve also read that at one point it was named The Eagle Hotel in the 1880’s, but I am not totally sure of a date on that.  Anyway, the old hotel was razed in 1924, and the new one was built in 1925.  It’s grand opening was June 17, 1925. The New Perry Hotel’s location was at the crossroads of two major paved roads at a time when real estate was booming in Florida and automobiles were starting to be mass produced.  This made for large numbers of tourists, and large numbers of tourists were good for a new hotel!

Ownership of the hotel has changed hands a number of times over the course of its many years in business.  I best remember the hotel when Yates and Nanette Green owned it.  They took it over in 1944 and ran it for 50 years. (Amazing!)  During that time the hotel and its famous restaurant really prospered.  In 2004, it became a registered historical site.

It was sold in 2011, but the hotel has never really gotten off the ground since the Greens left. This has been so sad to me.  You see, it used to have beautiful gardens with magnolias and azaleas and plenty of blooming annuals.  A motel section and a pool were built behind it.  You could look out over the gardens and pool from my favorite table in the windowed corner of the restaurant.  (This next photo is older than me!)

motelpostcards.blogspot.com

The best part of the hotel was the restaurant…southern cooking at its finest, served on starched white tablecloths.  I can’t even begin to tell you how many luncheons and wedding rehearsal dinners I attended there.  My bridesmaids luncheon was there. Nor can I tell you how many times I had their wonderful cream of chicken or turkey soup served in small white bowls.  The tables always always had a relish tray with bread and butter pickles, celery and carrot sticks, radishes, and these tiny hard crackers. (My husband called them rocks!)  Tea was served unsweetened with a tall spoon and the glasses stood in tiny dishes. (The photo below is a more recent one with water glasses at dinner.)

sweetfunkyvintage.blogspot.com

The lobby had a wonderful piano where Christmas music was played during the holidays.  (The hotel was even decorated and opened for a tour during one of Perry’s Christmas home tours.)

visitgeorgiaonline.com

Now I told you that the hotel had sold in 2011, but it has also been sold this year to new owners – a group of investors. They decided to liquidate the old building of as many items as they could (but they kept the piano. 🙂 )  My husband and I went to the liquidation sale back in June.  I just couldn’t pass up owning a bit of this place that holds so many heartstrings for me.  Here are a few photos of some of the rooms set up for the sale.  It was really sad to see the rooms like this….almost like we were going to an estate sale for someone who had passed away.

See those lights up there? They were for sale.  I could kick myself for not buying one of them. I could have painted those yellowed candle sleeves, and it would have been perfect in my workroom. 🙁

I know these have not been lovely photos for today’s post, but there is a reason for my showing them to you.  I wanted to tell you that there is good news about the hotel! 🙂  The group of investors who purchased it are intent on bringing the hotel and the restaurant back to their glory days and making the New Perry Hotel a centerpiece of the town again.  I don’t know exactly how they are going to do it, but I do know they have lined up James Farmer to be in charge of landscaping and interior design.  How’s that?!  You can watch him as he tells all about it here:
