More than 25 Recipes for S’mores and…the Summer Camp Package Giveaway Winner!


I thought we would wind up our Summer Camp series by celebrating with food.  How does that sound to you?  S’mores are the classic choice, but oh my goodness at all the recipes out there that have been inspired by them!  There are even whole cookbooks that are devoted entirely to their yummy goodness.


S’mores: Gourmet Treats For Every Occasion

So I have pulled together a number of recipes that are spin-offs of the original s’mores idea of graham crackers, chocolate bars, and toasted marshmallows. (Each of the links will take you straight to their recipe.) We’ll start with Martha’s recipe for them with a homemade graham cracker.

marthastewart.com

Next…a deliciously rich s’mores fudge bar.

handletheheat.com

Then we have s’mores cookies…

 s’mores cupcakes…

s’mores pizza triangles (that are like the s’mores pizzas we have at our local pizza buffet restaurant.)

iwashyoudry.com

And here is one that marries the taste of s’mores with the good old rice krispie treats recipe.  Talk about a dream dessert of childhood!

the-baker-chic.com

These mini s’more pies are supposed to be super easy, and I like that they are individual servings.

Version 1…

And finally, our daughter’s birthday is August 10th which also happens to be National S’mores Day! This is perfect because she is the queen of s’mores around here…roasting marshmallows over the stovetop every chance she gets.  I am thinking that for her birthday next year, we should try out this recipe for an inside-out s’mores cake.  Doesn’t it look divine?

hungryrabbitnyc.com

I want to thank everyone for reading the Summer Camp posts.  I enjoyed reading all 189 comments that got in before the close of the giveaway.  I especially loved the comments about your memories (good and bad!) of summer camp and your dream wedding ideas. And I want to do a BIG thank you to Missy in Knoxville  for her comment yesterday with the link to a gorgeous home remodeled by Johnson Architecture (check out the beautiful mirrored sconces in the living room and the fireplace in the kitchen…sigh) and her other link to a video on the charity of the winners of the Extreme Makeover Home Edition house from the last post. It is wonderful that their work for single mothers has now expanded into an entire village.  Be sure you read Missy’s comment on 8/23/13.

Let’s wind up our conclusion of the Summer Camp series by announcing the winner of the giveaway package.  Who was the lucky winner of the Found, Free, or Flea book by Teresa Surratt, (one of the owners of Camp Wandawega), the blu-ray version of Indian Summer, and a set of marshmallow roasting skewers from Williams Sonoma?

So congratulations to Kayleigh on winning (and on her engagement!)  Kayleigh, if you will email me your mailing address, I will get the package out to you asap.  I know you will like the book and movie, and I hope you will make some delicious s’mores to enjoy with the man of your dreams.

I hope everyone has a fun fantastic weekend!  Perhaps you would like to tackle this project? 🙂

August 20, 2013

Summer Camp – Camp Fish Lips, Johnson Architecture, and the giveaway continues

source

(photo by Peter Montanti mountainphotographics.com )

When I first started this series on summer camps, I was amazed at how many of you had spent your childhood summers at camps in the beautiful state of North Carolina. (North Carolina must have a lot of summer camps!) So today, we are leaving the Canadian lake houses that we’ve visited in our last few posts and heading to the South, but I’m afraid we won’t quite make it to North Carolina.  Instead, we are going to visit an architectural firm in Knoxville, Tennessee. (Does close count?)

Johnson Architecture has designed everything from restaurants to churches to libraries, and even a zoo! But this particular private home that they designed at Norris Lake is what caught my eye.  It is named Camp Fish Lips. 🙂  Doesn’t the exterior look just like something you would find at a summer camp?

jainc.com (Photography by Peter Montanti.)

Now look inside at that huge open room.  I can definitely feel the “summer camp vibe” going on in the design of this space.

jainc.com (Photography by Peter Montanti.)

There is even a canoe up there on those amazing rafters.houzz.com (Photography by Peter Montanti.)

No summer camp would be complete without bunkbeds (and there are screen doors outside the bedroom doors.)

houzz.com (Photography by Peter Montanti.)

And even the master bedroom has the feel of a summer camp.  I have always said that I would love to live in a glass house, and this room has enough windows to meet that desire.

houzz.com (Photography by Peter Montanti.)

I have scoured the internet for more photos of this home, but unfortunately I could not find any more.  But I did find a few more rooms and homes that Johnson Architecture has designed.  This porch and the following two rooms come from a vacation retreat they designed, and it was photographed by Beall and Thomas of Blackberry Farm.

houzz.com

houzz.comhouzz.com

I am not sure where this Johnson Architecture home is located (it is not the one from above), but that outdoor fireplace is gorgeous – and again very “summer campy.”  Care to roast some marshmallows?

jainc.com (Photography by Peter Montanti.)

Here is another porch they designed.houzz.com

I have a horrible fear of heights, and just looking at this photograph makes my head dizzy, but I thought this treetop walkway they designed was amazing (although I would never be able to take one step on it!)

jainc.com (Photography by Peter Montanti.)

While I was researching this architectural firm, I came across several interesting things about them.  First, they designed this…

facebook.com (Photography by Peter Montanti.)

a pavilion at The Estates at Norton Creek, near Gatlinburg, Tennessee.

Then I discovered that they also designed this (a perfect fit with summer camp posts)…

facebook.com

Camp Buck Toms…a Boy Scout camp!

And finally, I was thrilled to discover this….

facebook.com

Do you recognize it?  … No?  Well maybe this will help…

facebook.com

Hmmm…blue volunteer t-shirts… a big “Move that bus!” bus.  Figured it out, yet?  Yep…Extreme Makeover Home Edition!  Johnson Architecture was the design firm with the Knoxville Build of Extreme Makeover Home Edition.  They designed the home for the Watson family, and the show was a two hour special that aired last Thanksgiving.  The ABC write up of the episode is here if you would like to read it on their website,  and a video showing a Thanksgiving Day interview with architect, Daryl Johnson discussing his firm’s role in the show is here.

youtube.com

Isn’t it amazing how they can build a home of that size that quickly?!  Coordination of all the jobs must be incredible.

We have gone from a summer camp type house to a large scale home on television, and we’ve seen a Boy Scout camp in between.  I guess this post has been all over the place!  That just shows you the range of talent that Johnson Architecture must have.  I hope you have enjoyed seeing some of these spaces.  Remember if you leave a comment on any or all of the Summer Camp posts, you will be entered for the Thursday night drawing (8/22/13) of the summer camp package that includes these things:

Most of these spaces are more rustic in style than I would like for a year round home, but I think they are fun and fitting for a vacation, lake, or mountain home.  So my question for you to talk about today is..

Would you be happy with this rustic decor in your everyday permanent residence?

Looking forward to hearing from you!
