A Time for Rustic Porches

print

source

It is no secret.  I adore porches, and this time of year I want a porch that has a rustic feel…one where I can curl up on a swing with a blanket and a hot cup of cider.  So with that mood in mind (and since I have already shown you my porch here and here and here, 🙂 ) I thought we would take a look at some gorgeous porches that fit with the mood for fall.

This porch in Highlands, North Carolina was done by Nancy Braithwaite.  I have been looking for a solid gold throw like the one on the back of that sofa.  Have you seen one in that color anywhere?

nestegg.typepad.com

I love this log cabin that is also in Highlands.  What a great big wicker table to have for enjoying a meal outside.  If you click on the link, it will take you to the article from Traditional Home.  All of the rooms in this cabin are gorgeous, (and one of the drapery fabrics was my inspiration for the fabric in my master bath.)

traditionalhome.com

Amelia Handegan did this beautiful porch that I believe is also in a home in Highlands.  More wicker here…

pinterest.com

And this is another view of the same porch.myhomeideas.com

Another Amelia Handegan one is next. This time it is in South Carolina.  It has wicker again and a great stone fireplace.

myhomeideas.com

And yet another Amelia Handegan porch in South Carolina.  (Can you tell I like her work??)  The dark wicker, the black trim, that wonderful stone fireplace, the wood ceiling….I love it all.

myhomeideas.com

Now just to prove to you that a rustic porch does not have to be all stone and dark wood, here is a white porch that still has the cozy rustic feel.  It is dressed for fall.  Do you suppose that picture of the spider stays up year round?

countryliving.com

If you read my posts on Blackberry Farm a couple of weeks ago, you will remember I featured the original owners’ home, Toad Hall.  Here was the porch from that wonderful place.

architecturaldigest.com

And another view showing the fireplace there…hgtvremodels.com 

The current owners of Blackberry Farm have a new home, and this is the porch on that house (recently featured in Architectural Digest.)

architecturaldigest.com

The next two I want you to see are a little ahead of the season.  They are already dressed for Christmas, but they are such good examples of rustic porches I couldn’t leave them out. Just look beyond all the Christmas greenery for now, okay?

southernliving.com

traditionalhome.com

The last few I want to show you are from Cashiers, North Carolina – a little town just a few miles up the curvy road from Highlands.  This first one is by Kathleen Rivers and was just featured in Garden and Gun magazine.

gardenandgun.com

I really like the bright yellow confit pots and the simple lines of the table in this next porch.  It was featured in House Beautiful magazine.

housebeautiful.com

And finally, I saved my favorite until last.  This porch is also in Cashiers, North Carolina.  It was done by Phoebe Howard, and I think I like it because it is not only rustic, but also because it just seems so peaceful and calm in its simplicity.

phoebehoward.net

It wasn’t photographed in the fall, but it still feels cozy to me.  Maybe it is all the natural colors that are used in the space. (The plants on the porch make it look like spring, but the trees outside the house look like winter.)

phoebehoward.net

As I have looked at all of these I see a common thread of wicker furniture, natural or dark tones, and most have had a stone fireplace.  One day (when I win the lottery 🙂 ) I hope we will build a stacked stone fireplace on the outside wall of our back porch.  It’s the one big missing element for our home here, and it would make such a difference on a brisk fall or winter day.  We are major do-it-yourself-ers here, but building a fireplace is one thing we have not tackled.

But I am happy just to sit out there right now and enjoy a drink with a good book.  I hope you have the opportunity to enjoy some quiet porch time too!

until next time…
