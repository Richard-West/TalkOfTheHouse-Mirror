The Inn at Little Pond Farm

print

source

The other day when I was doing research for the post on Valle Crucis I came across an inn there that I had not been to – nor had I even heard of.  It is so beautiful, I just have to share it with you.  Take a look!

ncinn.com

 Owners Frank and Gaye Luaces have given new life to this 1900 farmhouse.  They are “Florida transplants” who spent eight years bringing European style to the Inn at Little Pond Farm.  The farmhouse is sited on five acres with evergreens and a meadow (and I would guess a pond, too.)

thecafesucrefarine.blogspot.com

There are European antiques throughout the inn in rooms with wide planked French oak floors.  Bead board, plank walls, and trim are all painted shades of gray, cream, and white.

bloomingbranch.blogspot.com

And the food is apparently wonderful, too!  Gaye learned French and Tuscan cooking while vacationing in Europe.  Look at this gorgeous kitchen with its marble countertops.  They even offer cooking classes if you would like to sign up for them while you are there.

ncinn.com

There are classes offered next week on Valentine’s Day and on a few Saturdays left in this month.  French cooking classes are available at Little Pond Farm on Saturdays in March and April (info here.)  Not only do they have culinary classes, but they also provide hiking picnics, and they host cookbook signings (although she is not French, I would love to see Sara Foster there!)  And by the way, they are a member of the James Beard Foundation.

ncinn.com

Let’s talk about your room now.  You will find European linens, pillows, and down comforters waiting on your bed.  Which of these rooms do you like?

cabinologist.files.wordpress.com

ncinn.com

bloomingbranch.blogspot.com

And then there are the luxurious bathrooms…heirloomphilosophy.blogspot.com

ncinn.com

ncinn.com

If you need a venue for your wedding, they can also handle that for you…indoors or out.ncinn.com

The daily rates include your breakfast, afternoon sweets, and early evening hors d’oeuvres with wine.  Doesn’t this sound like a lovely place to go at least for a weekend.  You could enjoy everything from fly fishing to horseback riding to simply taking it easy and relaxing in your room (sounds mighty tempting to me!)

thecafesucrefarine.blogspot.com

I have added this to my list of places I want to visit, and I even contemplated it for spring break.  But then I remembered something.  Many many years ago we went to Asheville, North Carolina for spring break.  It was early April – should have been very pretty in the mountains, but we got stranded in a record breaking snowstorm – 12 inches in 24 hours – not fun….hours and hours creeping along the interstate. I vowed never to go north for my spring break again.  Hopefully, we can visit the Inn at Little Pond Farm this summer.  It looks like a lovely place to me for a getaway!

Hope you enjoyed the tour.
